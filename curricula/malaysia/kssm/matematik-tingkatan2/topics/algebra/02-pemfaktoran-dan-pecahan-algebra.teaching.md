# Pemfaktoran dan Pecahan Algebra (Factorisation and Algebraic Fractions) — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This chapter is the core algebra manipulation unit in Form 2. Students connect expansion and factorisation as inverse processes, then apply these tools in combined operations. Accuracy, structure recognition, and operation order are key success drivers.

## RPT/DSKP Anchors
- Chapter: Bab 2 (Static RPT source, Week 4-6)
- SK: 2.1, 2.2, 2.3
- SP focus: 2.1.1-2.1.4, 2.2.1-2.2.3, 2.3.1-2.3.3

## Prerequisites Check
- Students can collect like terms and work with linear expressions (F1-05).
- Students can solve basic linear equations and verify results (F1-06).

## Teaching Sequence

### 1. Expansion Meaning and Mechanics (20 min)
Use area models and distributive property to connect geometric and symbolic expansion.

### 2. Factorisation as Reverse Expansion (25 min)
Start with common factor extraction, then move to structured forms. Keep each method tied to a recognition cue.

### 3. Combined Operations on Expressions (25 min)
Sequence tasks from simple to mixed operations to avoid overload. Emphasize operation order and clean line-by-line work.

### 4. Method Selection (20 min)
Present equivalent problems with different best methods; ask students to justify method choice before solving.

### 5. Application Problems (20 min)
Use contextual or non-routine items where students must both simplify and interpret outcomes.

## Common Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why It Happens | Remediation |
|---|---|---|
| Treats expansion and factorisation as unrelated topics | Topics taught as separate procedures | Pair every expansion example with its reverse factor form |
| Loses negative sign across brackets | Weak sign-discipline in multi-step algebra | Require sign-highlighting before each transformation |
| Cancels terms illegally across addition/subtraction | Overgeneralized fraction simplification habits | Use counterexamples and explain cancellation conditions explicitly |

## Engagement Hooks
- Method-match challenge: teams match problems to best solving strategy before calculation.
- "Spot the trap" board race with deliberate sign/cancellation mistakes.

## Assessment Guidance
- Include procedural fluency items and explanation-required items.
- Require at least one problem where students must choose between expansion or factorisation.
- Add one multi-step item with marks distributed by method + accuracy.
