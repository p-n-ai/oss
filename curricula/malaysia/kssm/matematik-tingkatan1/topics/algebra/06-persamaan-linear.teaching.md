# Persamaan Linear (Linear Equations) - Teaching Notes

## Overview
Chapter F1-06 develops equation-solving from one variable to two variables and then simultaneous equations. It is the bridge between symbolic manipulation and model-based problem solving.

## DSKP Coverage Snapshot
- 6.1 Linear equations in one variable
- 6.2 Linear equations in two variables
- 6.3 Simultaneous linear equations in two variables

## Prerequisites Check
- F1-05 algebraic expressions
- Rearranging arithmetic statements and inverse operations

## Teaching Sequence

### 1. One-variable equation concept (15 min)
- Distinguish equation vs expression.
- Identify characteristics of linear equations in one variable.

### 2. Forming equations from situations (20 min)
- Translate statements/contexts into equations.
- Validate whether formed equation matches the story.

### 3. Solving one-variable equations (25 min)
- Solve and check by substitution.
- Include short word-problem applications.

### 4. Two-variable equations and graphs (25 min)
- Form equations in two variables.
- Explore possible solutions and graph representations.

### 5. Simultaneous equations (25 min)
- Form simultaneous equations from daily contexts.
- Solve using substitution/elimination with interpretation.

## Common Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why it happens | Remediation |
|---|---|---|
| "Move across equals" without meaning | Procedure memorization | Use balance-model language before symbolic shortcuts |
| Dividing only one side | Weak equality concept | Keep two-column transformations for both sides |
| Stops after solving but no check | Habit gap | Require substitution check as final line |

## Engagement Hooks
- Ticket pricing with adult/student categories
- Mix-and-match school supplies cost problem
- Graph matching game for two-variable equations

## Assessment Guidance
- Sequence: identify -> form -> solve -> interpret
- Include one graph-reading item and one simultaneous-equation context item
