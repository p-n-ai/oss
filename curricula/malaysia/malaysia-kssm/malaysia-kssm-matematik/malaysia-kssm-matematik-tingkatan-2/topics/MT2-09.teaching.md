# Laju dan Pecutan — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic introduces students to the concepts of speed and acceleration, two fundamental aspects of motion in mathematics and physics. Students will learn to define speed as a rate involving distance and time, differentiate between uniform and non-uniform speed, and perform calculations involving average speed and acceleration. Additionally, students will tackle real-world problems to strengthen their understanding and application of these concepts.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should already be familiar with basic arithmetic operations, unit conversions, and the concept of rate from previous lessons on ratios and proportions.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Understanding Speed (20 min)
Introduce the concept of speed as a measure of how quickly an object moves from one place to another. Discuss the formula for speed: Speed = Distance / Time.
- **Strategies:** Use a speedometer chart and real-life examples like cars traveling on a highway.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What is the speed of a car that travels 100 km in 2 hours?"
- **The Trap:** Students may confuse speed with velocity. Clarify that speed is scalar, while velocity is vectorial.

### 2. Uniform vs. Non-uniform Speed (15 min)
Explain the difference between uniform speed (constant speed) and non-uniform speed (variable speed).
- **Strategies:** Use graphs to show constant vs. changing speed over time.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "How does a speedometer reading change when speed is non-uniform?"
- **The Trap:** Assuming speed is always constant, even in real-life scenarios.

### 3. Calculating Average Speed (20 min)
Guide students through calculating average speed using the formula: Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time.
- **Strategies:** Solve problems using step-by-step breakdowns, use unit conversion tables.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Calculate the average speed if a journey of 150 km takes 3 hours."
- **The Trap:** Forgetting to sum the total distance or time correctly.

### 4. Introduction to Acceleration (20 min)
Define acceleration as the rate of change of speed and introduce the formula: Acceleration = Change in Speed / Time.
- **Strategies:** Use animations showing cars speeding up and slowing down.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What is the acceleration if a car's speed increases from 20 m/s to 30 m/s in 5 seconds?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing acceleration with speed.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception               | Why Students Think This                          | How to Fix                                      |
|-----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Speed is the same as velocity | They both involve distance and time terms.     | Emphasize the directionality aspect of velocity. |
| Average speed is the average of speeds | Misinterpretation of "average" in context. | Use multiple examples to clarify calculation.   |

## Engagement Hooks
- Relate speed and acceleration to local sports such as cycling or motor racing events.
- Discuss the importance of speed limits on roads and how they are enforced.

## Assessment Guidance
- Use multiple-choice and open-ended questions to assess understanding.
- Include real-world problems that require unit conversions and reasoning to solve.
