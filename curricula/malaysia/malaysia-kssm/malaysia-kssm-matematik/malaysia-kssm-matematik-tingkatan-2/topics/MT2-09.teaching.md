### Speed and Acceleration — Teaching Notes

#### Overview
Fuh, this is where math starts moving for real! 🚴💨 From cycling races to cars on the highway, this chapter unlocks the secret behind how fast things move and how quickly they change speed. Prepare to mind-blow the student—this is not just numbers anymore, this is motion in action!

> [!IMPORTANT] **Chatbot Delivery Rules:**
> * **Bite-Sized Pacing:** Max 2 short paragraphs per message. Build concept step-by-step.
> * **Tone & Dwibahasa:** High-energy Malaysian tutor vibe. Mix terms like “Speed (Laju)” naturally.
> * **Golden Rule 1:** ALWAYS force unit checking before calculation (km/h vs m/s).
> * **Golden Rule 2:** ALWAYS separate Total Distance and Total Time before computing Average Speed.

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Teach each concept visually, numerically, and contextually.

##### 9.1 Speed
* **Strategies:**
  Start with real-life: “If you travel 100 km in 2 hours, how fast are you going?” Build the idea of rate = distance/time. Use road signs (90 km/h) to anchor meaning.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):**
  “If distance increases but time stays same, what happens to speed?”
* **The Trap:**
  Students confuse speed with distance. Reinforce: speed is a **rate**, not a total.

##### 9.1.2 Uniform vs Non-uniform Speed
* **Strategies:**
  Use graph visualization. Straight line = uniform speed, curve = non-uniform. Ask student to sketch both.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):**
  “Does a curved graph mean speeding up or slowing down?”
* **The Trap:**
  Students think any straight line = stationary. Clarify axes interpretation.

##### 9.1.3 Average Speed
* **Strategies:**
  Multi-stage journey. FORCE calculation:
  Total Distance ÷ Total Time  
  Use real scenario: school → shop → home.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):**
  “Why can’t we just average the speeds?”
* **The Trap:**
  The “(v1 + v2)/2” mistake. Destroy this misconception early.

##### 9.2 Acceleration
* **Strategies:**
  Define as change in speed over time. Use sprinter example: start → speed up → slow down.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):**
  “If speed is constant, what is acceleration?”
* **The Trap:**
  Negative acceleration confusion. Explain as “slowing down,” not something new.

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Speed = Distance | Misinterpret numbers | Use “rate per time” examples |
| Average Speed = Mean Speed | Misuse of averaging | Emphasize total distance/time |
| Acceleration always positive | Misunderstand sign | Use braking example |

  “Home Motion Experiment”
  Student measures time for object moving across floor, calculates speed, then changes conditions (push harder or incline) and explains acceleration change.
