Get ready — this is where geometry becomes real and meaningful. Circles are not just shapes; they are perfect systems of balance, symmetry, and hidden mathematical relationships. In this chapter, students will uncover the meaning of π, explore chord symmetry, and apply circle properties in real-world contexts like engineering, design, and architecture in Malaysia.

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 5.1 Parts and Construction of Circles
Introduce the anatomy of a circle: centre, radius, diameter, chord, arc, sector, and segment.
* **Strategy:** Use “pizza slice” analogy for sector and “cut cord” analogy for chord.
* **CFU (Check for Understanding):**
  “If a line does not pass through the centre, can it still be a diameter? Explain.”
* **Common Trap:** Confusing **sector vs segment** — remind students:
  sector = bounded by radii, segment = bounded by chord.

---

##### 5.2 Symmetry Properties of Chords
Show that circles are perfectly balanced structures.
A radius perpendicular to a chord always bisects it.

* **Strategy:** Use dynamic geometry tools (GeoGebra) to show symmetry in action.
* **CFU:**
  “If two chords are equal, must they be equally far from the centre? Why?”
* **Common Trap:** Forgetting that radius + chord always forms an **isosceles triangle**, which is useful for Pythagoras applications.

---

##### 5.3 Circumference and Area of a Circle
Introduce π as a constant ratio — not just a number, but a universal rule of circles.

* **Strategy:** Use string-unrolling method to derive circumference and area formulas.
* **CFU:**
  “If radius doubles, does area double or become four times bigger? Explain.”
* **Common Trap:** Mixing up radius and diameter in formulas — emphasize:
  circumference uses diameter or radius, but area ALWAYS uses radius squared.

---

#### Common Misconceptions
| Misconception | Fix |
|---|---|
| π = 3.14 exactly | π is irrational; 3.14 is only an approximation |
| Diameter is any long line | Must pass through centre |
| Sector = Segment | Sector uses radii, segment uses chord |
