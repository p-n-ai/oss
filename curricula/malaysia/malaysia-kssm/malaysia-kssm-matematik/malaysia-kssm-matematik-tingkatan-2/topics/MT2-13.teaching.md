# Kebarangkalian Mudah — Teaching Notes

## Overview
Kebarangkalian Mudah, or Simple Probability, is an introduction to the concept of likelihood and chance. Students will learn to calculate the probability of single events using simple fractions, helping them understand the fundamental principles of probability in everyday situations.

## Curriculum Standards & Taxonomy
- **Standard Code:** DSKP MT2-13

- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Understand the concept of probability as a measure between 0 and 1.
  - Determine the probability of simple events.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should be familiar with basic fractions and the concept of chance. They should also have experience with simple arithmetic operations such as addition and division.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Probability (10 min)
Introduce the concept of probability as a measure of how likely an event is to happen. Explain the probability scale from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).
- **Strategies:** Use a number line to visually demonstrate the probability scale. 
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask, "What does a probability of 0.5 mean?"
- **The Trap:** Students may think that probability can be more than 1.

### 2. Calculating Simple Probability (15 min)
Guide students through calculating the probability of a single event using fractions. Use examples like rolling a die or picking a card from a deck.
- **Strategies:** Use manipulatives like dice or decks of cards to visualize the process.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What is the probability of rolling a 3 on a six-sided die?"
- **The Trap:** Forgetting to consider all possible outcomes.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Probability over 1 | Misunderstanding the probability scale | Emphasize that probability is always between 0 and 1 |
| Probability as a whole number | Not recognizing probability as a fraction | Use visual aids to show probability as parts of a whole |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss the probability of rain in your local area and relate it to weather forecasts, encouraging students to consider how probability affects daily decisions.

## Assessment Guidance
When evaluating student understanding, look for the correct use of fractions and ensure they can explain their reasoning. Encourage students to verbalize their thought process and relate it back to real-world scenarios.
