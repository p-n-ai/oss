# Kebarangkalian Mudah — Teaching Notes

## Overview
Kebarangkalian Mudah, or Simple Probability, introduces students to the concept of likelihood and chance. This topic helps students understand how to calculate the probability of single events using simple fractions, laying the groundwork for more complex probability concepts later.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a solid understanding of basic fractions and be familiar with the concept of chance. They need to be able to perform simple arithmetic operations like addition and division confidently.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Probability (10 min)
Introduce probability as a measure of the likelihood that an event will occur, ranging from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). Use examples to illustrate this range.
- **Strategies:** Employ a number line to visually demonstrate the probability scale.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask, "What does a probability of 0.5 mean?"
- **The Trap:** Students might think probability can exceed 1.

### 2. Calculating Simple Probability (15 min)
Guide students through calculating the probability of a single event using fractions. Use practical examples like rolling a die or drawing a card from a deck.
- **Strategies:** Use manipulatives such as dice or card decks to illustrate the process.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What is the probability of rolling a 3 on a six-sided die?"
- **The Trap:** Forgetting to account for all possible outcomes.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Probability over 1 | Misunderstanding the probability scale | Emphasize that probability is always between 0 and 1 |
| Probability as a whole number | Not recognizing probability as a fraction | Use visual aids to show probability as parts of a whole |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss the probability of rain in your local area and relate it to weather forecasts, encouraging students to consider how probability affects daily decisions.

## Assessment Guidance
When evaluating student understanding, observe their use of fractions and ensure they can articulate their reasoning. Encourage students to verbalize their thought process and connect it to real-world scenarios.
