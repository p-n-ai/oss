#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 6.1 & 6.2: Shape Identity + Nets (The “Unfolding Reality” Stage)
* **Strategies:** Force students to mentally “peel” objects like durian → what 2D shapes appear?
  Use **GeoGebra / MS100 animation** to rotate and unfold shapes dynamically.
* **CFU:** “If I open a cylinder, what shapes appear?”  
* **The Trap:** Students think spheres have nets → NOPE ❌ cannot flatten sphere without distortion (like world map problem)

---

##### 6.3 Surface Area (The “Sum Everything” Rule)
* **Strategies:** Break every shape into nets → calculate EACH face → sum all areas  
  Emphasize cylinder rectangle length = **circumference (2πr)**  
* **CFU:** “Cone surface area uses slant height or vertical height?”  
* **The Trap:** Missing hidden faces in combined solids (like cylinder + hemisphere = overlap danger!)

---

##### 6.4 Volume (The “Space Inside” Logic)
* **Strategies:** Teach volume as BASE × HEIGHT stacking logic  
  Example: stack coins, stack water, stack rice sacks  
  Show cone/pyramid = **1/3 of prism/cylinder (critical KSSM insight)**  
* **CFU:** “How many cones fill 1 cylinder?” → answer: 3  
* **The Trap:** Students use slant height instead of vertical height → WRONG direction!

---

#### High Alert Misconceptions

| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Slant height used in volume | They confuse side edge with height | “Height is elevator (vertical), not staircase (slanted)” |
| Sphere has edges/vertices | Looks like it has poles | “Ball = smooth semua arah, no corners at all” |
| Forgetting hidden faces | Students only count visible sides | “Jangan lupa ‘tutup’ & ‘tapak’ — semua permukaan wajib kira!” |
