#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 12.1 Core Idea: The “Data Identity”
* **Strategies:** Position Mean, Median, Mode as “3 judges deciding what is normal.” Use school marks or Mamak bill context.
* **CFU:** “If your marks are 40, 50, 60, 100, which number best represents you?”
* **The Trap:** Students assume ONLY mean matters.

##### 12.2 Ungrouped Data Mastery
* **Strategies:** Force physical sorting: imagine students lining up numbers like school assembly.
* **CFU:** “Can you find median WITHOUT sorting? Try and explain why it fails.”
* **The Trap:** Forgetting ordering step → wrong median.

##### 12.3 Extreme Value Shock (Outlier Effect)
* **Strategies:** Add “billionaire student” into class dataset to show mean distortion.
* **CFU:** “Why does median stay stable but mean changes?”
* **The Trap:** Thinking mode is affected by outliers.

##### 12.4 Grouped Data Logic
* **Strategies:** Introduce class intervals + midpoint as “representative student”.
* **CFU:** “Why do we use midpoint instead of exact values?”
* **The Trap:** Confusing modal class vs highest frequency.

---

#### High Alert Misconceptions

| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Mode = Frequency | Confuse value vs count | Mode is the “winner item”, not the number of votes |
| Median needs no sorting | Skip ordering step | “No sort, no median — like no queue, no entry!” |
| Mean is always best | Over-trust formula | Mean is sensitive to extreme values (outlier distortion) |
