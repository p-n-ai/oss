# Perimeter dan Luas — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic covers the fundamental concepts of perimeter and area, focusing on various geometric shapes. Students will learn to calculate, estimate, and solve problems involving these two aspects. The topic is designed to build foundational skills in geometry, essential for further mathematical studies.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of geometric shapes and measurement units. Familiarity with addition and multiplication is also essential to facilitate calculations.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Perimeter (20 min)
Introduce the concept of perimeter as the 'border' of a shape.
- **Strategies:** Use a rope to outline shapes on the floor, then measure with a ruler.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What is the perimeter of this shape?"
- **The Trap:** Students might confuse perimeter with area; emphasize "border" vs. "space."

### 2. Estimating and Measuring Perimeter (20 min)
Teach how to estimate and accurately measure perimeter.
- **Strategies:** Use grid paper to draw and measure shapes.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "How close was your estimate to the actual measurement?"
- **The Trap:** Overlooking units of measurement; stress consistency.

### 3. Introduction to Area (20 min)
Discuss area as the 'space' within the boundary of a shape.
- **Strategies:** Use colored tiles to fill shapes and count the number.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "How many tiles fit inside the shape?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing area with perimeter; reiterate "space" vs. "border."

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Perimeter is the same as area | Both involve measurements of a shape | Use visual aids to distinguish 'border' from 'space' |
| Larger perimeter means larger area | Larger outside doesn't always mean larger inside | Provide counterexamples with differing shapes |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss how farmers use perimeter to fence their land and area to calculate planting space, using local agricultural examples.

## Assessment Guidance
Assess understanding through practical activities, such as measuring and calculating the perimeter and area of classroom objects. Use quizzes that include estimation and problem-solving questions.
