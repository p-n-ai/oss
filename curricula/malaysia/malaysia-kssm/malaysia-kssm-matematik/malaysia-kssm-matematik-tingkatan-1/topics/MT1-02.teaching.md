#### Overview

Fuh, prepare to mind-blow the student! This chapter is the ultimate "DNA laboratory" of mathematics. We are going to teach the student how to break numbers down into their fundamental building blocks (Factors) and how to predict where they will meet in the future (Multiples). Without this, they will be lost in the "jungle" of fractions and algebra later on!

#### Prerequisites Check

*   **Multiplication Tables (Sifir):** If they don't know their sifir, multiples will be a nightmare!
*   **Prime Numbers:** They must remember that prime numbers only have two factors: 1 and itself (e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7).

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

**Langkah 1: The DNA of Numbers (SK 2.1.1 - 2.1.2)**
*   **Strategies:** Use "Exploration Activity 1" to show factors as numbers that divide completely. Introduce **Prime Factorisation** using the **Factor Tree** or **Repeated Division** methods.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If I have 12 donuts, can I share them exactly with 5 friends? No? Then is 5 a factor of 12?".
*   **The Trap:** Many students think 1 is a prime factor because it's a factor of everything. Tell them: "1 is a factor, but NOT a prime factor because 1 is not a prime number!".

**Langkah 2: Finding the "Boss" Divisor (HCF/FSTB) (SK 2.1.3 - 2.1.5)**
*   **Strategies:** Use the construction worker scenario—finding the biggest square tile to cover a floor. Teach **Repeated Division** where we only divide by common factors.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "We found common factors 1, 2, and 4. Which one is the 'Big Boss' HCF?".
*   **The Trap:** Students often stop dividing too early. Remind them: "Keep going until NO MORE prime numbers can divide ALL the numbers at the same time!".

**Langkah 3: The Leap of Numbers (LCM/GSTK) (SK 2.2.1 - 2.2.3)**
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Neon Lights" or "Bus Timetable" scenario. Use an **Electronic Spreadsheet** to show how multiples grow and overlap.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Bas A comes every 10 mins, Bas B every 15 mins. When is the first time they meet? That's your LCM!".
*   **The Trap:** Confusing HCF and LCM. Remind them: "Factors (DNA) are smaller or equal to the number. Multiples (Leaps) are larger or equal!".

#### High Alert Misconceptions

| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| "Factors are larger than the number." | They confuse factors with multiples. | Use the 'DNA Rule': DNA stays *inside* the body, so it must be smaller or equal! |
| "HCF must be a big number because it says 'Highest'." | The word "Highest" is misleading. | Clarify: HCF is the *Biggest Divider* (which makes numbers smaller). LCM is the *Smallest Meeting Point* (which makes numbers larger). |
| "All odd numbers are prime numbers." | Because 3, 5, 7 are prime. | The "Number 9 Trap": Show them that 9 is odd, but $3 \times 3 = 9$. So 9 is NOT prime! |
