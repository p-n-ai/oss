# Faktor dan Gandaan — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic introduces students to the fundamental concepts of factors and multiples, focusing on identifying and working with factors, prime factors, common factors, and least common multiples. It's a building block for understanding number properties and arithmetic operations.

## Curriculum Standards & Taxonomy
- **2.1.1 (Understand):** Identify and list factors of whole numbers to generalize about factors.
- **2.1.2 (Apply):** Identify and list prime factors and express numbers in prime factorization.
- **2.1.3 (Understand):** Explain and identify common factors of whole numbers.
- **2.1.4 (Apply):** Determine HCF (Highest Common Factor) for two or three whole numbers.
- **2.1.5 (Apply):** Solve problems involving HCF.
- **2.2.1 (Understand):** Explain and identify common multiples of whole numbers.
- **2.2.2 (Apply):** Determine LCM (Lowest Common Multiple) for two or three whole numbers.
- **2.2.3 (Apply):** Solve problems involving LCM.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should be familiar with basic multiplication and division facts, understand the concept of a number line, and have a grasp of whole numbers.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Factors (15 min)
Introduce the concept of factors by using simple numbers like 6 and 12. Explain that factors are numbers you can multiply together to get another number.
- **Strategies:** Use arrays and factor trees to visually display factors.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask, "What are the factors of 12?" Then wait for responses.
- **The Trap:** Students might confuse factors with multiples; emphasize multiplication vs. division context.

### 2. Prime Factorization (20 min)
Demonstrate breaking down numbers into their prime factors using factor trees.
- **Strategies:** Use color-coded charts to separate prime numbers from composites.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Prompt, "Can you show the prime factorization of 18?"
- **The Trap:** Forgetting to include all prime factors; ensure students double-check their trees.

### 3. Finding Common Factors and HCF (20 min)
Guide students to find common factors of two numbers, leading to identifying the highest common factor.
- **Strategies:** Utilize Venn diagrams to visually compare factors.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask, "What is the HCF of 8 and 12?"
- **The Trap:** Mistaking HCF for LCM; clarify by highlighting differences.

### 4. Understanding Multiples and LCM (20 min)
Explain multiples and guide students in finding the least common multiple using lists or grids.
- **Strategies:** Employ number lines to illustrate multiples.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Pose, "What is the LCM of 4 and 5?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing sequences; practice listing multiples correctly.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Factors are larger than the number | Larger numbers seem more significant | Reinforce factors as divisors, smaller or equal |
| Prime factorization is unique | Confusion with composite numbers | Use multiple examples to show uniqueness |

## Engagement Hooks
- Use real-world scenarios like distributing equally among friends or setting up seating arrangements to illustrate factors and multiples.

## Assessment Guidance
Use quizzes with a mixture of identification and application problems. Prioritize feedback on problem-solving approaches and encourage peer reviews for deeper understanding.
