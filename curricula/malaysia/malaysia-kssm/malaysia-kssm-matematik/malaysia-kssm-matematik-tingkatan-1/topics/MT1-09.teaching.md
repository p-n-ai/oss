#### DSKP Anchors & Taxonomy
*   **SK 9.1:** Polygons (Poligon).
    *   **SP 9.1.1:** State the relationship between the number of sides, vertices and diagonals of polygons.
    *   **SP 9.1.2:** Draw polygons, label vertices of polygons and name the polygons based on the labelled vertices.
*   **SK 9.2:** Triangles (Segi tiga).
    *   **SP 9.2.1:** Recognise and list geometric properties of various types of triangles. Hence classify triangles based on geometric properties.
    *   **SP 9.2.2:** Make and verify conjectures about (i) the sum of interior angles, (ii) the sum of interior angle and adjacent exterior angle, (iii) the relation between exterior angle and the sum of the opposite interior angles of a triangle.
    *   **SP 9.2.3:** Solve problems involving triangles.
*   **SK 9.3:** Quadrilaterals (Sisi empat).
    *   **SP 9.3.1:** Describe the geometric properties of various types of quadrilaterals. Hence classify quadrilaterals based on the geometric properties.
    *   **SP 9.3.2:** Make and verify the conjectures about (i) the sum of interior angles of a quadrilateral, (ii) the sum of interior angle and adjacent exterior angle of a quadrilateral, and (iii) the relationship between the opposite angles in a parallelogram.
    *   **SP 9.3.3:** Solve problems involving quadrilaterals.
    *   **SP 9.3.4:** Solve problems involving the combinations of triangles and quadrilaterals.

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 9.1 Polygons (The Foundation)
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Arkitek Bijak" persona. Direct the student to use **GeoGebra** (File: `Diagonals of polygon.ggb`) to discover that Number of Vertices = Number of Sides. Introduce the diagonal formula: $\frac{n(n-3)}{2}$.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If a polygon has 10 sides (decagon), how many vertices does it have? Don't count, just guess!"
*   **The Trap:** Students often think a diagonal can be a side. Warn them: "A diagonal ONLY connects non-adjacent vertices! If they are neighbors, it's just a side!".

##### 9.2 Properties of Triangles
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Folding Method" visualization for axes of symmetry. Verify conjectures using `Triangles properties of angles.ggb`.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If I have an isosceles triangle (segi tiga sama kaki) and one base angle is $70^\circ$, what is the other base angle? Why?".
*   **The Trap:** The "Exterior Angle" confusion! Students often forget that the exterior angle is the sum of the TWO opposite interior angles ($d = a + b$), NOT just any angle outside.

##### 9.3 Properties of Quadrilaterals
*   **Strategies:** Compare and contrast shapes using a "Family Tree" (e.g., A Square is a special Rhombus). Verify that all interior angles of a quadrilateral sum to $360^\circ$ using `Interior angles of quadrilateral.ggb`.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "A parallelogram has one angle of $100^\circ$. What is the angle directly opposite it? (Hint: They are twins!)".
*   **The Trap:** The "Trapezium Symmetry" Trap! Remind them that most trapeziums have NO axes of symmetry unless they are "Isosceles Trapeziums".

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| All polygons must be "perfect" (regular). | Textbooks often show regular shapes first. | Show an "Irregular Decagon" that looks like a monster. Call it a "Broken Star" to prove it still has 10 sides!. |
| Exterior angles can be any size. | They don't see the "Straight Line" extension. | The "Jalan Terus" Rule: Force them to draw a dotted line extending from the side. The angle MUST stay on that line. |
| A Rhombus is just a tilted Square. | Both have 4 equal sides. | The "90-degree Check": A Square is a Rhombus with a "perfect posture" ($90^\circ$). A Rhombus can be "senget" (tilted). |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The Mosque Mystery:** Look at the Putra Mosque. Ask: "Can you spot the pentagons and hexagons in the dome? Without these polygons, the whole building would collapse!"
*   **Nature's Math:** "Why do bees use hexagons for honeycombs instead of circles? (Hint: It's about saving wax and space!)".

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Rule - The Labeling Point:** In drawing questions (SP 9.1.2), students lose marks if vertices are not labeled in alphabetical order (clockwise or anticlockwise).
*   **UASA Rule - Show Your Working:** For combined shapes (SP 9.3.4), always state the property used (e.g., "Sum of angles in a triangle = $180^\circ$") before calculating.
*   **UASA Rule - The 'Straight Line' Hint:** Look for keywords like "PQR is a straight line" to find hidden $180^\circ$ relationships.
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** **"The Paper Architect Challenge"** – Instruct the student to take an A4 paper and fold it to create a perfect Equilateral Triangle without using a ruler. They must text back the number of axes of symmetry they found and explain *why* the angles are all $60^\circ$.
