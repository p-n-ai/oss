We are going to destroy the fear of negative numbers, fractions, and decimals by treating them as everyday "rakyat" problems like Mamak debts, temperature drops, and diving deep in the ocean.

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 1.1 Integers
*   **Strategies:** Force the student to visualize! Use the **Lift Analogy**: "Imagine you're at Aras G. You go down 2 floors to the basement. Where are you? B2! That is -2, boss!".
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If a submarine is at 50m below sea level, how do we write that as a number? Is it +50 or -50?".
*   **The Trap:** The **"Bigger is Better"** trap. Students think -10 > -2 because 10 is bigger than 2. Remind them: "Better to have RM2 debt (-2) or RM10 debt (-10)? -2 is actually 'richer' than -10!".

##### 1.2 Basic arithmetic operations involving integers
*   **Strategies:** Use the **"Pasar Malam Handshake"** for the Distributive Law: $a(b + c) = ab + ac$. The 'a' must shake hands with both 'b' and 'c'. For operations, use colored chips (Yellow for +, Red for -) to represent zero pairs.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Try this: what is $-5 - (-3)$? Don't let the double negative trick you!".
*   **The Trap:** The **Double Negative Panic**. Students forget that $-(-x)$ becomes $+x$. Force them to rewrite the signs first before calculating.

##### 1.3 Positive and negative fractions
*   **Strategies:** Use the **Number Line** visual. Show that negative fractions move to the left of zero just like integers. Use **GeoGebra software** for dynamic exploration of fraction placement.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Which is smaller? $-3/4$ or $-1/4$? Think of the number line position!".
*   **The Trap:** The **"Naked Numerator"** trap. When subtracting fractions, remind them to use brackets around the numerator so the negative sign is distributed correctly.

##### 1.4 Positive and negative decimals
*   **Strategies:** Carry over the **Number Line** visual. Show that negative decimals follow the exact same rules as integers and fractions. Use **GeoGebra software** to zoom in between integers to find the decimals.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Which is smaller? $-0.5$ or $-0.7$? Think of the number line position!".
*   **The Trap:** The **"Decimal Alignment"** panic. Remind them to align the decimal point when adding or subtracting in standard form.

##### 1.5 Rational numbers
*   **Strategies:** Reveal the **Secret Identity**. Every number that can be written as $a/b$ (where $b \neq 0$) is in the "Rational Numbers Club".
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Is the number 5 a rational number? Can you write it as a fraction?".
*   **The Trap:** The **"Zero Destroyer"**. Students might try to divide by zero. Warn them: "Dividing by zero is like a Syntax Error that destroys the universe! Never let $b=0$!".

#### High Alert Misconceptions

| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| "Bigger is Better" (-10 > -2) | They ignore the negative sign and only see the absolute value. | **Debt Logic:** "Would you rather owe RM10 or RM2? -2 is closer to zero, so it's 'larger'!". |
| Zero Denominator (a/0 is okay) | They think zero is just another number. | **The Pizza Error:** "Can you share 5 pizzas with 0 people? It's impossible! The universe will crash!". |
| "Decimals aren't Rational" | They only focus on the 'fraction' definition and forget conversions. | **The Club Rule:** "If it can be a fraction (like $0.5 = 1/2$), it's in the club!". |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The Mamak Debt Hook:** "You go Mamak, order Roti Telur (RM2.50) but only have RM2. Boss says 'kasi hutang lah'. You are now at -RM0.50. Welcome to integers!".
*   **Climate Change Crisis:** Discuss the Paris 2015 Summit. If global temperatures rise even $1^\circ C$, glaciers melt. Use negative numbers to represent temperatures at the poles.
*   **Submarine Warfare:** A submarine dives -50m and shoots a flare +100m into the air. What is the total distance?.

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA OAP (Multiple Choice):** Focus on identifying non-rational numbers (e.g., dividing by zero).
*   **UASA OPB (Mapping/True-False):** Use number line ordering for mixed types (e.g., arrange -0.5, 3/4, -2, 1).
*   **UASA Subjective:** Real-world problem solving involving bank balances or temperature changes over several days.
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini:** **"Household Number Line!"** Ask the student to find 3 items at home (e.g., a spoon, a remote, a coin). Tell them to draw a long line on paper with '0' in the middle. Place the coin at -2, the spoon at +1.5, and the remote at -0.5. They must snap a photo, upload it, and explain why the coin is "poorer" than the remote.
