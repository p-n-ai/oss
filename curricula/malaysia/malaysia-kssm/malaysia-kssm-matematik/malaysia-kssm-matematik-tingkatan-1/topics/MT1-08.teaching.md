# Garis dan Sudut — Teaching Notes

## Overview
In this topic, students will explore the fundamental concepts of lines and angles. They will learn to identify and describe congruence in line segments and angles, estimate and measure sizes, and describe angle properties. Students will also gain skills in constructing lines and angles and solving related problems using these concepts.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should be familiar with basic geometry concepts such as points, lines, and angles. Prior knowledge of measuring angles with a protractor will be beneficial.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Lines and Angles (20 min)
Introduce students to basic line and angle terminology and concepts. Discuss what congruence means in geometry.
- **Strategies:** Use visual aids like diagrams and models to illustrate different types of lines and angles.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask students to identify examples of congruent angles in the classroom.
- **The Trap:** Students may confuse congruence with similarity. Emphasize exactness in size and shape for congruence.

### 2. Measuring and Estimating (25 min)
Guide students in using tools like rulers and protractors to measure line segments and angles accurately.
- **Strategies:** Hands-on activities where students measure objects around the classroom.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Have students estimate and then measure angles, comparing results.
- **The Trap:** Students may misread protractors or measure from the wrong angle point.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| All angles are measured from the top of the protractor. | Students may not understand that protractors have two scales. | Demonstrate using both scales and practice with various angles. |
| Congruent angles have to be in the same orientation. | Students might visualize congruence visually rather than by measurement. | Use physical manipulatives to show angles in different orientations. |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss how angles are used in local architecture, such as the Petronas Towers, to create stability and design.
- Explore how lines and angles are crucial in designing traditional batik patterns.

## Assessment Guidance
- Use practical tasks where students must construct or identify angles and lines in physical or real-world contexts.
- Provide scenarios where students must determine missing angles on intersecting lines or parallel lines cut by a transversal.
