#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 8.1 Lines and Angles
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Eraser Estimation" technique. Ask the student to estimate the length of a line segment by comparing it to an object (like an eraser) before measuring. For constructions, guide them through **GeoGebra** or physical compass steps.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If I rotate a 40° angle, does it stay 40°? Why?" This tests their grasp of **Congruency (Kekongruenan)**.
*   **The Trap:** The "Long Arm" illusion. Students often think an angle is bigger if the lines (arms) are longer. Remind them: "Size doesn't matter for arms, only the opening counts!".

##### 8.2 Angles Related to Intersecting Lines
*   **Strategies:** Visualization of the "X" shape. Use sweeping arrows to show **Vertically Opposite Angles (Sudut Bertentang Bucu)** are equal.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Give them one angle in an "X" intersection and ask them to find the other three in under 10 seconds.
*   **The Trap:** Students forget that **Adjacent Angles (Sudut Bersebelahan)** on a straight line must sum to 180°.

##### 8.3 Parallel Lines, Transversals, and Real-World Angles
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Letter Logic." Look for "F" for **Corresponding Angles (Sudut Sepadan)**, "Z" for **Alternate Angles (Sudut Selang-seli)**, and "C" or "U" for **Interior Angles (Sudut Pedalaman)**.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Use the "Bird and Cat" analogy for **Angle of Elevation (Sudut Dongak)** and **Angle of Depression (Sudut Tunduk)**. Ask: "If Jefri looks at the bird, is that elevation or depression?"
*   **The Trap:** Thinking the angle of elevation and depression are measured from the vertical line. "Always start from the **Horizontal Line (Garis Mengufuk)**, or you'll fall into the trap!".

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Protractor Scale Confusion | They just read the first number they see on the scale. | "Check the 'Zero-Start'! Is your base line on the left or right? Start counting from 0, not 180!" |
| Orientation = Congruency | They think the angle 'changes' if you turn the paper. | Use the "Mobile Phone" analogy. If you rotate your phone, the screen size doesn't change, right? Same for angles! |
| Elevation from Vertical | They measure from the wall/tree instead of the ground. | "Eyes to the Horizon! Always draw your flat 'invisible line' first before looking up or down." |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The Penang Bridge Stability:** Discuss how the cables of the Penang Bridge form specific angles to distribute weight. "Without the right angles, the bridge goes boom!".
*   **The Batik Master:** Explore the geometric precision in **Batik patterns**. Traditional artists are actually "hidden mathematicians" using symmetry and angles.
*   **Career Focus (Surveyors):** Introduce how land surveyors use angles to map out Malaysia’s skyscrapers without ever climbing them.

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Rule - Construction:** Ensure the student can use a compass and ruler ONLY for 60° angles and perpendicular bisectors. No protractors allowed for these!.
*   **UASA Rule - Problem Solving:** Test the ability to find $x$ and $y$ using multiple properties (e.g., combining parallel lines with intersecting lines).
*   **UASA Rule - Real World:** Questions will involve a person looking at an object; students must draw the horizontal line and label the angle correctly.
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** **"The Geometry Scout Challenge."** Ask the student to find one "Parallel" and one "Intersecting" line in their house (e.g., window frames, floor tiles). They must take a photo, use a markup tool to draw the angles, and tell you if they are **Complementary (Sudut Pelengkap)** or **Supplementary (Sudut Penggenap)**. "Upload your masterpiece to prove you're a Master of Angles!".
