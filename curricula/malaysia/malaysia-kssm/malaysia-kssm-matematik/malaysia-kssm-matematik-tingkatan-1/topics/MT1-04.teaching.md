#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 1. The Art of Comparison: Ratios (SP 4.1.1 - 4.1.3)
*   **Strategies:** Force the student to visualize "The Order". Use the *Hippocampus kuda* (seahorse) context from page 74. Explain that a ratio has no units—it’s just a pure comparison!
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If I have 2 weeks and 16 days, can I write the ratio as 2 : 16 immediately? Think carefully about the units!".
*   **The Trap:** The "Unit Chaos" trap! Warn them they MUST convert to the same unit before writing the ratio, or the whole thing is *salah*!.

##### 2. Speed and Density: Rates (SP 4.2.1)
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Walking through Time" hook. Show them that a Rate (Kadar) is a special ratio comparing two different units (like km/h or RM/kg).
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Is 1.5 kg / 3 g a rate? Let's discuss!".
*   **The Trap:** The "Simplification Slip-up". When converting rates (e.g., m/s to km/h), students often multiply when they should divide. Force them to write the units in a "fraction bridge".

##### 3. The Balanced Scale: Proportions (SP 4.3.1 - 4.3.2)
*   **Strategies:** Introduce the "Unitary Method" vs. "Cross-Multiplication". Use the electricity cost example from the textbook (Example 8) to show how proportions keep things fair.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If 3 balls cost RM5, how much for 12 balls? Try to use the proportion method: $\frac{5}{3} = \frac{x}{12}$".
*   **The Trap:** The "Upside Down" trap! Students often swap the numerator and denominator on one side of the equation. Remind them: "Keep your units in the same 'rumah' (house) on both sides!".

##### 4. The Grand Connection (SP 4.4.1 - 4.5.3)
*   **Strategies:** This is the boss level! Connect Ratios to Percentages and Decimals. Use the "Alternative Method" (multiplying by 100%) for quick conversions. Anchoring: Use the *Vitruvian Man* to show human body proportions.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If 30% of your pocket money is saved, what is the ratio of savings to total money? Write it in simplest form!".
*   **The Trap:** The "Whole vs Part" confusion. Students often confuse the ratio of "Part : Part" with the fraction of "Part / Whole". Always ask: "Are we comparing to the total or the other group?".

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Ratios = Fractions | Both look like $a/b$. | Show them a ratio of 1:2 (part to part) is a fraction of 1/3 (part to whole). |
| Order doesn't matter | 2:3 feels the same as 3:2. | Use a recipe! "If you swap the ratio of sugar to salt, your *Bubur Pulut Hitam* is ruined!". |
| Units in Ratios | Habit from other topics. | "Ratios are naked!" They compare the same kind of thing, so units cancel out. |

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Exam Rules:** Expect "Objective Pelbagai Bentuk" (OPB) like matching equivalent ratios. Subjective questions will often require the "Cross-Multiplication" step-by-step working.
*   **Mathematical Fikrah:** Demand the 4-step problem-solving cycle for TP5 questions (Understand, Plan, Solve, Verify).
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** **"The Vitruvian You!"** Ask the student to find a photo of themselves or a family member. Have them measure the height of the head and the total body height. Ask them to calculate the ratio. Is it close to Leonardo da Vinci's $\frac{2}{5}$ ratio? Have them text back their measurements and their conclusion!.
