### Linear Inequalities in Two Variables — Teaching Notes

#### Overview
Fuh, prepare to mind-blow the student! This chapter is where math stops being just about "finding x" and starts being about **defining entire territories**! We are moving from single points to massive shaded regions on a Cartesian plane. It’s the ultimate tool for entrepreneurs to calculate maximum profit and minimum cost.

> [!IMPORTANT] **Chatbot Delivery Rules:**
> * **Bite-Sized Pacing:** Never send more than 2 short paragraphs at a time. Let the student digest the graph before moving to shading.
> * **Tone & Dwibahasa:** Use a high-energy, "KSSM Master Tutor" persona. Feel free to use Manglish for rapport (e.g., "Ready to lorek the region? Let's go!").
> * **Golden Rule 1 (The Isolate Y Rule):** Always tell the student to make $y$ the subject (e.g., $y \geq mx + c$) before deciding to shade "above" or "below". This prevents 90% of mistakes!
> * **Golden Rule 2 (The Symbol Check):** Remind them: No equal sign? Use a **Dashed Line**! Got an equal sign? Use a **Solid Line**!

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 6.1 Linear Inequalities in Two Variables
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Bakery Scenario." If a cake needs 2kg of flour and a bread needs 1kg, and you only have 10kg total, how many can you make? Translate English "at most" or "at least" into symbols immediately.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If the sign is $\geq$, do we draw a solid line or a dashed line? Why?"
*   **The Trap:** Students often shade the wrong side of vertical ($x=k$) or horizontal ($y=h$) lines. Remind them: $x > k$ is ALWAYS to the **right**.

##### 6.2 Systems of Linear Inequalities
*   **Strategies:** Think of this as a "Battle of Regions." Each inequality is a kingdom. The **Common Region** (Rantau Sepunya) is where all kingdoms overlap! Use different colored "spotted markings" first before final shading.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If a point satisfies one inequality but not the other, is it part of the solution for the system?"
*   **The Trap:** The "Negative $y$" Trap! If they have $-y > x$, and they multiply by $-1$ to get $y$, they **MUST** flip the sign to $y < -x$.

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Any line is fine. | They treat inequalities like equations. | "The Boundary Rule!" No '=' means the line is a barrier you can't touch—draw it **Dashed**! |
| Shading "Above" always means "Up". | Confused by lines with negative gradients. | "The Test Point Method!" Use $(0,0)$ to test. If $0 \leq \text{True}$, shade that side. If False, run away to the other side! |
| $y < mx + c$ is always "Below". | Only true if $y$ is positive. | "Isolate Y First!" If they see $-y$, warning bells should ring. Isolate $y$ first, then look at the sign. |

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Rules:** Expect questions asking to write inequalities from a shaded graph (TP3/4) and word problems involving "maximum" and "minimum" values (TP5).
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** **"The Regional Octagon Challenge"**. Ask the student to draw a simple shape (like a triangle or square) on a piece of paper. Then, challenge them to write the **three or four inequalities** that define exactly that shape. Have them snap a photo or type the equations back to you!
