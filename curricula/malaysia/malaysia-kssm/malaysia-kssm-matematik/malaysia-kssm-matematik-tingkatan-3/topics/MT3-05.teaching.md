#### Overview
Prepare to mind-blow the student! This chapter isn't just about triangles; it's the secret language of architects and navigators. We are bridging the gap from simple Pythagoras to the abstract power of **Nisbah Trigonometri** (Trigonometric Ratios).

> [!IMPORTANT] **Chatbot Delivery Rules:**
> * **Bite-sized pacing:** Max 2 short paragraphs per message. Let the student breathe!
> * **The "Manglish" Bond:** Use encouraging, casual Malaysian tutor vibes. "Ready to tackle this? Jom!"
> * **Golden Rule #1:** Never let a student calculate without identifying the **Reference Angle** first.
> * **Golden Rule #2:** SOH-CAH-TOA is the holy grail. Make them chant it!

#### DSKP Anchors & Taxonomy
*   **5.1.1 (Remember/TP1):** Identify opposite side, adjacent side, and hypotenuse based on an acute angle.
*   **5.1.2 (Understand/TP2):** Make and verify conjectures about relationships between angles and side ratios to define sine, cosine, and tangent.
*   **5.1.3 (Analyze/TP4):** Verify the impact of changing angle sizes on ratio values.
*   **5.1.4 (Apply/TP3):** Determine the values of sine, cosine, and tangent of acute angles.
*   **5.1.5 (Apply/TP3):** Determine values for 30°, 45°, and 60° without a calculator.
*   **5.1.6 (Apply/TP4):** Perform calculations involving sine, cosine, and tangent (using $\sin^{-1}$, etc.).
*   **5.1.7 (Create/TP5 & TP6):** Solve complex and non-routine real-world problems.

#### Prerequisites Check
*   **MT1-13 Pythagoras Theorem:** Students must be "pro" at $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ before they touch Sin/Cos/Tan.
*   **Angle Basics:** Identifying right-angled triangles ($90^\circ$) and acute angles ($< 90^\circ$).

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 1. The Orientation Phase (Identifying Sides)
*   **Strategies:** Use "The Staring Contest" analogy. Ask the student to "stand" on the angle $\theta$. The side they are looking directly at is the **Opposite Side**. The side they are "sitting" on (that isn't the long hypotenuse) is the **Adjacent Side**.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If I move the angle from the bottom corner to the top, does the Hipotenus change? Why or why not?" 
*   **The Trap:** Students thinking the "bottom" side is always the Adjacent side. Remind them: "It all depends on where $\theta$ is standing, lah!".

##### 2. The Discovery Phase (Defining Ratios)
*   **Strategies:** Use the **Brainstorming 1** method. Force the student to visualize a triangle growing larger but keeping the same angle. Use **GeoGebra** to show that while sides get longer, the ratio stays constant!.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If we double the size of the triangle but keep the angle at $30^\circ$, does the Sine value double? Tell me your logic."
*   **The Trap:** Thinking that bigger triangles mean bigger ratios. Explain that ratios are "loyal" to the angle, not the size.

##### 3. The Calculation Phase (Special Angles & Tools)
*   **Strategies:** Use the **Smart Finger** method for calculators. For special angles (30, 45, 60), use the "Split Equilateral Triangle" visualization instead of just memorizing tables.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Without a calculator, what is $\tan 45^\circ$? Think about a square cut in half!".
*   **The Trap:** Forgetting to switch the calculator to 'DEG' mode or misrounding minutes to degrees.

##### 4. The Application Phase (Problem Solving)
*   **Strategies:** Apply the 4-step cycle: Understand, Plan (SOH-CAH-TOA), Solve, Verify. Use the river-width or flagpole scenario to ground the math.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If you know the height of a tree and the angle you're looking up, which ratio helps you find your distance from the tree?"
*   **The Trap:** Using Sine when they should use Tangent. Always check: "Do we have the Hipotenus or not?"

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Adjacent is always the "floor" | They only see triangles sitting flat. | Rotate the triangle! Show them the "Adjacent" can be a vertical wall. |
| $\sin \theta$ means $\sin \times \theta$ | They treat 'sin' like an algebraic variable. | Tell them: "Sin without an angle is like a car without a driver. It goes nowhere!" |
| Ratios are proportional to angles | If $30^\circ = 0.5$, then $60^\circ$ must be $1.0$. | Use the calculator to debunk this instantly. Show it's a curve, not a straight line. |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The Spy Gadget:** Discuss how a **Theodolite** works to measure the height of mountains or the width of the Klang River without getting wet!.
*   **Historical Hype:** Introduce **Al-Battani**, the Father of Trigonometry. He compiled the first table of cotangents—trig is part of Islamic mathematical heritage!.

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Rule #1:** Focus on finding unknown lengths in 2D and basic 3D shapes (cuboids/pyramids).
*   **UASA Rule #2:** Ensure students can convert between degrees/minutes ($1^\circ = 60'$).
*   **UASA Rule #3:** Test the "Inverse" ($\sin^{-1}$) to find angles when sides are given.
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** **"The Household Clinometer"**. Ask the student to roll a piece of paper into a tube and tape a protractor to it with a string/weight. They must use it to measure the angle of elevation of a ceiling fan or a door frame, measure their distance, and then text back the calculated height using Tangent. They must explain their "Plan" before sending the final height.
