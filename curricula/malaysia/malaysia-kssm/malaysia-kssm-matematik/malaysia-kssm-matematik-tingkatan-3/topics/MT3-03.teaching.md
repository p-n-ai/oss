#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 1. The Banking Menu (3.1.1)
Introduce Savings (Akaun Simpanan), Fixed Deposits (Simpanan Tetap), and Current Accounts (Akaun Semasa).
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Mamak Menu" analogy. Just like choosing between Teh O and Teh Tarik, each bank account has a different "flavor" (interest rate and liquidity).
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If you want to save for a laptop in 2 years and don't want to touch the money, which account is the 'king'?"
*   **The Trap:** Students thinking Current Accounts (Akaun Semasa) pay high interest. Remind them it’s for business/cheques and often has no interest!.

##### 2. The Battle of Interests (3.1.2)
Compare Simple Interest (Faedah Mudah) vs. Compound Interest (Faedah Kompaun).
*   **Strategies:** Force the student to calculate interest for Year 1, then use that total as the *new* principal for Year 2. "See? The money is 'birthing' its own money!"
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If $n=4$ in the compound formula, how many times a year is the bank checking your balance?".
*   **The Trap:** The "P" confusion. In simple interest, $P$ stays the same. In compound interest, the "New Principal" grows every period!.

##### 3. Credit Card Detective (3.2.1 - 3.2.3)
Understanding the hidden costs of "plastic money".
*   **Strategies:** Show a mock statement of RM5,000 balance. Calculate the 5% minimum payment (RM250) and show how the remaining RM4,750 gets hit with 18% interest. "It’s a trap!".
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Why is paying only the minimum payment like trying to empty a swimming pool with a spoon?".
*   **The Trap:** Thinking credit cards are "free money." Emphasize that Credit = Debt that hasn't been paid yet!.

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Savings = Investment | Both involve putting money away. | Savings is for "Safety & Liquidity"; Investment is for "Growth & Risk". |
| $n$ is the total years | Confused with $t$. | $n$ is the "Compounding Frequency" (e.g., monthly $n=12$, quarterly $n=4$). |
| Minimum payment is enough | The bank says it's "OK" to pay that. | Show them the calculation: at 18% interest, you'll be paying for 20 years!. |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The Millionaire Path:** Use a compound interest calculator to show how saving just RM5 a day from age 15 can result in a massive sum by age 60.
*   **The Car Loan Choice:** Compare Bank A (low rate, long term) vs. Bank B (higher rate, short term). Which one actually costs less in total?.

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Rules:** Focus on $I=Prt$ for Section A (MCQ) and multi-step ROI or Loan comparisons for Section C (Subjective).
*   **Step-by-Step Working:** Marks are given for correctly identifying $P, r,$ and $t$ before the final answer.
*   **Financial Literacy EMK:** Questions should involve real-world scenarios like "Encik Zainal's credit card statement".
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** "The Budgeting Battle." Ask the student to find one real item they want to buy (e.g., a phone for RM2,000). They must "interview" a parent or check a website to find a "personal loan" rate and a "savings account" rate. They must text back a 12-month plan: "How much must I save monthly vs. what is the total cost if I take a loan?"
