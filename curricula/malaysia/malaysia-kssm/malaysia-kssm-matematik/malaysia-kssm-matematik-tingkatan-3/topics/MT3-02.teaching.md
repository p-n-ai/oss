#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 2.1 Significant Figures
*   **Step 1: The Art of Precision (Page 32-34)**
    *   **Strategies:** Use the "Brainstorming 1" context. Ask the student if estimating a shirt price at RM25 when it's RM28.70 is "good enough." Force them to see that **Accuracy (Kejituan)** depends on the number of **Significant Figures (Angka Bererti)** used.
    *   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "I have 0.0050140. If I tell you there are 7 significant figures, am I right or am I trying to 'kena' you? Why?"
    *   **The Trap:** The "Zero Hero" Trap! Students think all zeros count. Remind them: leading zeros for decimals are just placeholders—they are NOT significant!
*   **Step 2: The Essential Trim (Page 35-36)**
    *   **Strategies:** Guide the student through Example 2. Use the "Look Next Door" rule. If the digit after the cut-off is $\ge 5$, we +1. If it's $< 5$, leave it. 
    *   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Let's round RM 63,479 to 2 significant figures. Do we get RM 63,000 or RM 63,500? Explain your 'logic' to me!"
    *   **The Trap:** The "Vanishing Whole Number" Trap. When rounding integers (like 63,479), students often forget to replace the dropped digits with zeros to maintain the number's "size." 63,479 $\neq$ 63!

##### 2.2 Standard Form
*   **Step 1: The $A \times 10^n$ Code (Page 37-39)**
    *   **Strategies:** Show them the "Power of 10" shift. Force the student to physically count the decimal "jumps." For big numbers, the index is positive (it's "growing"). For tiny decimals, the index is negative (it's "shrinking").
    *   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "A single nanometre is $1 \times 10^{-9}$ m. How many zeros are we hiding behind that negative 9 index? Let's count together!"
    *   **The Trap:** The "$A \ge 10$" Mistake. Students often write $12.5 \times 10^3$. Stop them! $A$ must be $1.25$. If $A$ moves, the index MUST change!
*   **Step 2: Operations & Problem Solving (Page 40-47)**
    *   **Strategies:** For Addition/Subtraction, teach the "Index Alignment" strategy. Use the **Factorisation (Pemfaktoran)** method as the gold standard. For Multiplication/Division, link back to Form 2 Laws of Indices.
    *   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "We have $(3 \times 10^5) \times (4 \times 10^2)$. If I tell you the answer is $12 \times 10^7$, what 'illegal move' did I forget to fix?"
    *   **The Trap:** The "Index Mismatch" Trap. Students often add indices during addition (like coefficients). Remind them: We only add indices when mendarab (multiplying)!

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Zeros at the end of an integer are always significant. | They count all digits they see. | Refer to "Tahap Kejituan" (Accuracy Level). Unless specified, trailing zeros in a whole number like 803,000 are usually placeholders. |
| $10^{-3}$ means the number is negative. | They confuse the index sign with the number's sign. | No lah! Negative index means the number is "small/decimal," not negative. Show them $0.001$. |
| Adding $2 \times 10^3 + 3 \times 10^4 = 5 \times 10^7$. | They add coefficients AND indices. | Use the "Apples and Oranges" rule. You can't add different powers of 10 until the indices are the same! |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The Universe in your Hand:** Compare the distance to the moon (384,400 km) with the size of a virus (1 micrometre). How many viruses would fit between here and the moon? This shows why **Bentuk Piawai** is a superpower.
*   **The Hard Disk Battle:** Discuss the "1 Terabyte" hard disk. How many "bytes" is that in **Standard Form**? ($1 \times 10^{12}$ bytes). Use this to ground the "Tera" prefix from the textbook.

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Exam Rules:** Focus on Paper 1 (OAP) for quick conversions and Significant Figure identification. Paper 2 requires full working for **Operations (TP3)** and **Problem Solving (TP4/5)**.
*   **Calculator Mastery:** Ensure students know how to use the `Sci` mode on their scientific calculators to verify their answers.
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6 - Creative):** "The Shoebox Architect." Ask the student to find a rectangular object at home (like a shoebox or a phone). 
    1. Measure the length and width in cm.
    2. Calculate the area in $cm^2$.
    3. **The Challenge:** Convert that area into $m^2$ and state it in **Standard Form** correct to 3 **Significant Figures**.
    *Student must text back the measurements and their final Standard Form answer with reasoning.*
