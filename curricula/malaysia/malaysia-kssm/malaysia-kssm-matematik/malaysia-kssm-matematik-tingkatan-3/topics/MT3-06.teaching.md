This is where math starts looking like art—and students will need "Golden Eyes" to spot the hidden 90-degree angles and supplementary pairs!

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

**1. Circle Angles & The "Double" Rule (20 min)**
Introduce the relationship between central and circumference angles.
*   **Strategies:** Use "Mathematical Representation" by asking the student to imagine a bow and arrow—the arc is the bow, and the angle is where the arrow is pulled.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If the central angle (*Sudut Pusat*) is 100 degrees, how much is the angle at the *Lilitan*? Don't get it flipped!".
*   **The Trap:** Students thinking any angle inside the circle is the central angle. Remind them: It MUST touch the *Pusat* (center)!.

**2. The Cyclic Quadrilateral Secret (25 min)**
Focus on the 180-degree supplementary relationship.
*   **Strategies:** Visualization—ask the student to draw any 4-sided shape where all corners touch the "edge." 
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "In a *Sisi Empat Kitaran*, if one corner is 80 degrees, what is the value of the corner sitting directly opposite it? Think supplementary!".
*   **The Trap:** Thinking all 4-sided shapes inside a circle are cyclic. "Only if ALL four vertices touch the *Lilitan*!".

**3. Tangent Touchpoints & The 90-Degree Law (25 min)**
Introduce tangents and their property with the radius.
*   **Strategies:** The "Perfect Balance" analogy—like a bicycle wheel touching the road at a single point.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Why does the *Jejari* (radius) and *Tangen* always make a 90-degree angle? What happens if it was 89 degrees?".
*   **The Trap:** Misunderstanding a *Perentas* (chord) as a tangent. "A tangent is just a 'touch,' not a 'cut'!".

**4. Alternate Segments & Common Tangents (30 min)**
Integrate complex properties for higher TPs.
*   **Strategies:** Use digital visualization (GeoGebra) to show how the alternate segment angle "jumps" across the chord.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Look at the triangle inside the circle touching the tangent. Can you see which angle matches the one outside? It’s like a mirror reflection!".
*   **The Trap:** Misidentifying the alternate segment. "Always follow the chord to the opposite side!".

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Central = Circumference | They look 'pointy' and share the same arc. | The "Parent-Child" fix: The *Pusat* (center) is the 'Parent' (Big/Double), the *Lilitan* is the 'Child' (Small/Half). |
| Any 4-sided shape = Cyclic | If it's inside the circle, it must be cyclic. | The "Four-Point Check": If even one corner is 'floating' (not on the line), the 180-degree magic is broken!. |
| Tangent passes through circle | Confusing it with a *Perentas* (chord). | The "Mamak Table" fix: A tangent is like a flat table touching a round plate. It only touches one point!. |
