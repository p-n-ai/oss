#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 4.1.1 & 4.1.2: The Meaning of Scale (35 min)
*   **Strategies:** Show a map of Malaysia on a phone. Ask: "If this map was the real size, would it fit in your pocket?" Explain that Scale is a ratio. Force them to visualize $1:n$ as "1 unit on paper represents $n$ units in real life."
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If a map says 1:50,000, does 1cm on the map mean 50,000km in real life? Tell me why or why not."
*   **The Trap:** The "Size vs. Ratio" trap. Students think a larger $n$ means a larger drawing. Remind them: "A bigger $n$ means we shrunk the object more!"

##### 4.1.3: Calculating Dimensions (25 min)
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Cross-Multiply Method." Set up $\frac{\text{Drawing}}{\text{Object}} = \frac{1}{n}$. Instruct the student to draw a box around the units to ensure they match before calculating.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Give them a scale of 1:200 and a drawing length of 5cm. Ask: "Calculate the real length in meters. Show me your steps one by one!"
*   **The Trap:** The "Unit Chaos." Students forget to convert cm to m at the end. Warn them about the "Naked Numerator" in the ratio!

##### 4.1.4: Drawing to Scale (30 min)
*   **Strategies:** Use grids! Guide them to redraw a shape on $0.5\text{ cm}$ vs $1\text{ cm}$ grids. Mention using **GeoGebra** or digital grid paper to see how the shape stays "similar" but changes size.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If you draw a $2\text{ cm}$ line with scale 1:3, how long is your line on the paper? Is it getting bigger or smaller?"
*   **The Trap:** Distorted Proportions. Students change the length but forget the angles. Remind them: "Angles stay the same, only the sides change!"

##### 4.1.5: Problem Solving (20 min)
*   **Strategies:** Use the 4-step cycle: Understand (Identify scale/given), Plan (Formula), Solve (Calculate), Verify (Logical check). Use real contexts like the Merdeka 118 or a football field.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Give a complex problem involving speed/time (e.g., p. 99, Example 14). Ask: "What is the very first unit you need to convert here?"
*   **The Trap:** "The Inversion Trap." Using $n:1$ instead of $1:n$. Always check if the final answer makes sense (Is a real house really $5\text{ cm}$ wide?).

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bigger $n$ = Bigger Drawing | They see a large number and assume "Enlargement." | "Think of a Map! A huge forest (Object) must fit on a small paper (Drawing), so $n$ must be big to shrink it!" |
| Scale = Physical Size | Confusion between ratio and the length of the line. | "Scale is the 'Magic Shrink Ray' setting, not the actual ruler measurement." |
| Forgetting Units | They treat $1:200$ as just numbers without units. | "Units are the 'IC Number' of math. Without units, the number has no identity! Always $1\text{ cm} : n \text{ cm}$." |
