#### Overview
Fuh, prepare to mind-blow the student! We are diving into **Plans and Elevations**, the ultimate battleground where 3D reality meets 2D precision! From the Diamond Building in Putrajaya to the blueprints of a dream house, this chapter is where students learn to see the world like architects and engineers.

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### Subchapter 7.1: Orthogonal Projections
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Shadow Analogy." Imagine the sun is directly overhead (normal) and the shadow is the projection. Force the student to identify the **Normal to the Plane** using Example 1.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If you look at a cylinder from the top, what shape is the orthogonal projection? A circle or a rectangle?".
*   **The Trap:** The "Perspective Trap!" Students often draw what they see (3D) instead of the 2D projection. Remind them: Orthogonal = $90^\circ$ perpendicular!

##### Subchapter 7.2: Plans and Elevations
*   **Strategies:** Introduce the **Four-Quadrant Method**. Show them how the **Plan** sits in the bottom quadrant, with the **Front Elevation** above it. Use thin construction lines to link the views.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Why do we use a $45^\circ$ line in the third or first quadrant? What does it transfer?".
*   **The Trap:** The "Hidden Edge" disaster. If an edge exists but is blocked by a surface, it **must** be a dashed line. Check page 184 for the "TIPS" box!.

##### Subchapter 7.2.2: Synthesis & Problem Solving
*   **Strategies:** Work backwards! Give them the 2D views and ask them to "build" the 3D object in their mind. Use Example 9 as a guide.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If the Front Elevation is a triangle and the Plan is a square, what 3D object are we looking at?"
*   **The Trap:** Scaling errors. Students often forget to convert $1\text{ m}$ to $1\text{ cm}$ on paper if the scale is $1:100$.

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Projection is 3D** | Confused with perspective sketches. | Use a torchlight to show how a 3D object's shadow on a wall is strictly 2D. |
| **Solid vs Dashed** | Laziness or lack of visibility. | "The X-Ray Rule": If your eyes can't see it but the edge is there, you need X-ray vision (dashed lines)!. |
| **Quadrant Chaos** | Mixing up Front and Side views. | Follow the arrow directions $X$ and $Y$ strictly. $X$ usually = Front, $Y$ = Side. |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The Diamond Building Challenge:** Show a photo of the Energy Commission building in Putrajaya. Ask: "How would an architect draw the top view of this diamond shape?".
*   **Mimar Sinan's Secret:** Share how the great Ottoman architect used plans as benchmarks for mosque designs.

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Rules:** Expect Subjective questions requiring full-scale drawings on grid paper.
*   **KBAT Focus:** Synthesis of objects from plans (TP4/5) and volume calculations of the resulting solids.
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** "The Shoebox Architect." Ask the student to take a small box (like a tissue box) and draw its **Plan** and **Elevations** on paper. Then, they must "modify" the box by cutting a corner and redraw the new projections. They must upload a photo of the drawing and the modified box!.
