From the heights of Form 5 students to the daily Covid-19 cases in Malaysia, students will learn to see the patterns in the chaos!

#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 1. The Visual Groundwork: Histograms & Polygons
Teach the student that grouped data needs "breathing room" (class intervals). Focus on textbook pages 198-202.
*   **Strategies:** Use a "Digital Spreadsheet" analogy. Ask them to imagine sorting 1,000 students by height. 
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If a class is 150-154, what is the *Sempadan Atas* (Upper Boundary)? Is it 154 or 154.5?"
*   **The Trap:** Plotting the Frequency Polygon (*Poligon Kekerapan*) using class limits instead of midpoints! Warn them to always find the center of the bar first.

##### 2. The S-Curve: Ogives & Quartiles
Moving to cumulative frequency. Focus on pages 207-210.
*   **Strategies:** "The Cumulative Climb." Show how frequencies "stack up" like a staircase to form the Ogive.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "To find the Median position, do we use $N/2$ or $(N+1)/2$ for an Ogive?" (Correct answer: $N/2$).
*   **The Trap:** Forgetting the "Zero Frequency" start. If they don't start the Ogive at the lower boundary of the first class, it's floating in mid-air!

##### 3. The Math of Consistency: Variance & Standard Deviation
Calculation phase. Focus on pages 211-212.
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Athlete Comparison" scenario. Two archers both average 8 points, but Archer A hits (8, 8, 8) and Archer B hits (10, 8, 6). Who has the higher *Sisihan Piawai* (Standard Deviation)?
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If the variance is 229.19, how do we get the standard deviation? Square it or Square Root it?"
*   **The Trap:** Using the wrong formula for $\bar{x}$. Remind them to use $\sum fx / \sum f$, not just the average of class midpoints.

##### 4. The Summary: Box Plots
Final data representation. Focus on pages 213-214.
*   **Strategies:** The "Five-Number Summary" (Min, Q1, Q2, Q3, Max). Visualize the box as the "Heart" of the data.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If the median line is closer to the left side of the box, is the data symmetric or skewed?"
*   **The Trap:** Thinking the whiskers represent the whole range including outliers.

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Histograms = Bar Charts | Both use bars. | "Bar charts have gaps because they are 'single' (discrete). Histograms are 'besties' (continuous)—no gaps between boundaries!" |
| Plotting Ogive against Midpoints | Confused with Frequency Polygons. | "Ogive is about cumulative 'up to' a point. So we MUST use the Upper Boundary (*Sempadan Atas*)." |
| Range is enough | It's easy to calculate. | "Range only looks at the outliers. *Sisihan Piawai* looks at every single data point. It's the 'CCTV' of data!" |

#### Engagement Hooks
*   **The "Flatten the Curve" Mystery:** Show the Covid-19 graph from page 197. Ask: "How did the MCO change the *Standard Deviation* of daily cases? Did it make the spread narrower or wider?"
*   **The Durian Weight Challenge:** Imagine weighing 50 *Musang King* durians. If the Standard Deviation is high, can you trust the price-per-durian?

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
*   **UASA Rule 1:** Section B/C often asks to construct a histogram or polygon. Ensure they use a sharp pencil and check the scale (usually 2 cm to $x$ units).
*   **UASA Rule 2:** Interpret "Consistency." If Question A has $\sigma = 0.5$ and Question B has $\sigma = 1.2$, Question A is always more "consistent/stable."
*   **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** **"The BMI Spread."** Ask the student to collect the height and weight of 5 family members/friends. Guide them to calculate the BMI, then "fake" 15 more data points to create a frequency table. Have them sketch a Box Plot on paper, snap a photo, and explain if their "community" is healthy or skewed towards "Overweight."
