#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 1. The Unit Circle & Quadrant Signs (30 min)
Introduce the four *Sukuan* (Quadrants) and the "Add Sugar To Coffee" (All-Sin-Tan-Cos) mnemonic.
*   **Strategies:** Force the student to visualize a "sweeping arm" moving anti-clockwise from the positive x-axis. Ask them to imagine the y-coordinate as "height" (Sine) and x-coordinate as "width" (Cosine).
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If our angle is $210^\circ$, which *Sukuan* is he chilling in? And is the Sine value going to be positive or negative?"
*   **The Trap:** Students thinking Sine is always positive. Remind them: "In the 3rd Quadrant, even Sine feels negative!".

##### 2. Reference Angles & Calculation (20 min)
Convert any large angle into its "mini-me" acute version.
*   **Strategies:** Use the "Bow-Tie" visualization. Every reference angle $\alpha$ must "hug" the x-axis, never the y-axis.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Find the *Sudut Rujukan Sepadan* for $315^\circ$. Remember, we want the shortest path to the x-axis!"
*   **The Trap:** Calculating $315^\circ - 270^\circ = 45^\circ$. **Wrong!** Always subtract from $180^\circ$ or $360^\circ$, never $90^\circ$ or $270^\circ$.

##### 3. The Wave Riders: Graphing Functions (40 min)
Plotting the "DNA" of trig functions.
*   **Strategies:** Use **GeoGebra** to show why Tangent has "walls" (asymptotes) at $90^\circ$ and $270^\circ$. It's because the "width" (Cosine) becomes zero, and you can't divide by zero!.
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Look at the Cosine graph. Where does it start at $0^\circ$? Is it a 'Valley' or a 'Mountain Peak'?".
*   **The Trap:** Drawing the Sine and Cosine curves as "pointy" triangles. Remind them: "These are smooth waves, like the Langkawi tides, not shark teeth!".

##### 4. Parameter Transformers ($a, b, c$) (30 min)
How $a, b,$ and $c$ change the "vibe" of the wave.
*   **Strategies:** 
    *   $a$ = Amplitude (The volume knob - makes it louder/taller).
    *   $b$ = Period (The speed knob - more waves in the same space).
    *   $c$ = Vertical Shift (The elevator - moves the whole party up or down).
*   **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If I change $y = \sin x$ to $y = 3\sin x$, did the wave get wider or taller?".
*   **The Trap:** Thinking that a larger '$b$' makes the wave longer. **Nope!** Larger $b$ means the period $T = \frac{360^\circ}{b}$ gets smaller (squashed).

#### High Alert Misconceptions

| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| $\tan 90^\circ = 0$ | Confusing it with $\sin 0^\circ$. | "Dividing by Zero Error!" Show them on a calculator; it's *Tidak Tertakrif* (Undefined). |
| Reference angle can be $> 90^\circ$. | Measuring from the wrong axis. | "The X-Axis Hug!" Always measure the angle from the horizontal x-line. |
| Amplitude exists for Tangent. | Applying Sine rules to Tangent. | "Infinity has no limit!" Tangent goes to heaven and hell ($+\infty, -\infty$); it has no peak. |
