# Nisbah dan Graf Fungsi Trigonometri — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic explores the trigonometric ratios and functions, particularly focusing on how to analyze, apply, and create trigonometric graphs. Students will investigate the properties and behaviors of sine, cosine, and tangent functions across different quadrants and learn to draw and analyze their graphs over a complete cycle.

## Curriculum Standards & Taxonomy
- **6.1.1 (analyze):** Recognize and validate conjectures about sine, cosine, and tangent values in quadrants II, III, and IV.
- **6.1.2 (apply):** Determine sine, cosine, and tangent values based on reference angles.
- **6.1.3 (apply):** Determine angles given sine, cosine, and tangent values.
- **6.1.4 (apply):** Solve problems involving trigonometric ratios.
- **6.2.1 (apply):** Draw and compare graphs of trigonometric functions.
- **6.2.2 (create):** Analyze effects of parameter changes on trigonometric graphs.
- **6.2.3 (apply):** Solve problems using trigonometric function graphs.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should be familiar with basic trigonometric concepts, the unit circle, and how to calculate sine, cosine, and tangent for angles in the first quadrant. They should also understand the Cartesian plane and basic graphing skills.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Exploring Trigonometric Ratios (30 min)
Introduce the concept of reference angles and how they apply to quadrants II, III, and IV.
- **Strategies:** Use a unit circle diagram to demonstrate reference angles and their corresponding trigonometric values.
- **CFU:** "What is the sine of 150° if the reference angle is 30°?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing positive and negative signs of trigonometric values in different quadrants.

### 2. Drawing Trigonometric Graphs (40 min)
Guide students through the process of sketching y = sin x, y = cos x, and y = tan x for 0° ≤ x ≤ 360°.
- **Strategies:** Use graphing paper to plot points and highlight periodicity and amplitude.
- **CFU:** "How does the graph of y = sin x differ from y = cos x?"
- **The Trap:** Misplacing key points like maximum and minimum on the graph.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Sine, cosine, and tangent are always positive. | Students may generalize from the first quadrant. | Reinforce the concept of the unit circle and sign changes in each quadrant. |
| The graphs of sine and cosine are identical. | They notice the similar wave shapes. | Highlight phase shift and differences in starting points. |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss how trigonometric functions model real-world phenomena such as sound waves and tides in local coastal areas.

## Assessment Guidance
To assess understanding, provide problems that require students to draw graphs and solve real-world problems using trigonometric functions. Focus on their ability to correctly identify reference angles and apply transformations to the graphs.
