# Nisbah dan Graf Fungsi Trigonometri — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic introduces students to the trigonometric ratios of sine, cosine, and tangent, focusing on angles in the second, third, and fourth quadrants. Students will also learn to plot and analyze trigonometric function graphs, understanding how changes in parameters affect the graphs.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should be familiar with the basic trigonometric ratios for angles in the first quadrant and understand the unit circle concept. They should also know basic graphing techniques.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Understanding Sine, Cosine, and Tangent in Different Quadrants (30 min)
Introduce the idea of reference angles and how trigonometric values change signs in different quadrants.
- **Strategies:** Use a unit circle diagram to visually show angle positions and corresponding trigonometric values.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask students which trigonometric function remains positive in the second quadrant.
- **The Trap:** Confusing the signs of trigonometric ratios in different quadrants.

### 2. Calculating Trigonometric Values (20 min)
Guide students to calculate the sine, cosine, and tangent for angles in the second, third, and fourth quadrants.
- **Strategies:** Practice converting angles to reference angles and determining their trigonometric values.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Have students calculate the cosine of 150°.
- **The Trap:** Forgetting to apply the correct sign based on the quadrant.

### 3. Graphing Trigonometric Functions (40 min)
Teach students how to graph y = sin x, y = cos x, and y = tan x for 0° ≤ x ≤ 360°.
- **Strategies:** Use graph paper and graphing calculators to plot points and visualize the wave nature of graphs.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask what the maximum value of y = sin x is.
- **The Trap:** Misplacing the period and amplitude on the graph.

### 4. Analyzing Parameter Changes (30 min)
Explore how changes in parameters a, b, and c affect the trigonometric graphs.
- **Strategies:** Use dynamic graphing software to manipulate parameters and observe trends.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** What happens to the graph of y = a sin bx when a is increased?
- **The Trap:** Incorrectly attributing changes in graph shape to the wrong parameter.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| The value of sine, cosine, and tangent is always positive. | Students may only remember the first quadrant properties. | Reinforce the concept of the unit circle and signs in each quadrant. |
| The graphs of sine, cosine, and tangent are linear. | Lack of exposure to wave patterns. | Use graphing tools to demonstrate the periodic nature. |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss navigation and GPS technology relying on trigonometric calculations.
- Relate trigonometric graphs to sound waves and music production.

## Assessment Guidance
- Encourage students to explain the reasoning behind their calculations and graph interpretations.
- Use real-life scenarios for problem-solving to test understanding.
