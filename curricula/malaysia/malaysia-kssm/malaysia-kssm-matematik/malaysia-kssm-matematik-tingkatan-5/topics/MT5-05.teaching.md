# Kekongruenan, Pembesaran dan Gabungan Transformasi — Teaching Notes

## Overview
In this topic, students will delve into the world of congruence, enlargement, and combined transformations. They'll learn to distinguish between congruent and non-congruent shapes using sides and angles. Students will also explore geometric similarity, understand the concept of enlargement, and apply these transformations to solve real-world problems. Additionally, the topic covers tessellation, allowing students to creatively design repeating patterns using transformations.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of geometric shapes, angles, and simple transformations such as translation, reflection, and rotation.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Understanding Congruence (20 min)
Introduce the concept of congruence with simple shapes, focusing on equal sides and angles. Explain that congruent shapes are identical in size and shape, not just similar.
- **Strategies:** Use physical models and interactive geometry software to provide hands-on experience.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** “Can you find two congruent shapes in this set?”
- **The Trap:** Confusing congruent with similar; emphasize identical size and shape.

### 2. Exploring Similarity and Enlargement (25 min)
Discuss how similar shapes maintain proportionality and how enlargement affects dimensions while keeping angles constant.
- **Strategies:** Employ visual aids like grid overlays to show enlargement effects.
- **CFU:** “What stays the same and what changes during enlargement?”
- **The Trap:** Forgetting that angles remain constant during enlargement.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Congruent means similar | Both involve shapes | Clarify the difference between size and proportionality |
| Enlarged shapes have larger angles | Visual illusion | Use examples to demonstrate angle constancy |

## Engagement Hooks
- Use local architecture or art that features tessellations or transformations to spark student interest and show practical applications.

## Assessment Guidance
Assess understanding through practical tasks where students must identify or create congruent shapes, enlarged figures, or tessellations. Incorporate both written assessments and hands-on activities to evaluate comprehension.
