# Kekongruenan, Pembesaran dan Gabungan Transformasi — Teaching Notes

## Overview
In this topic, students will explore the concepts of congruence, enlargement, and combined transformations. They will learn to identify congruent shapes based on sides and angles, understand geometric similarity, and solve practical problems using these transformations. The topic also covers tessellation, providing a creative outlet for students to apply transformations in designing repeating patterns.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of geometric shapes, angles, and simple transformations like translation, reflection, and rotation.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Understanding Congruence (20 min)
Introduce the concept of congruence with simple shapes and highlight the importance of equal sides and angles.
- **Strategies:** Use physical models and interactive geometry software.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** “Can you find two congruent shapes in this set?”
- **The Trap:** Confusing congruent with similar; emphasize identical size and shape.

### 2. Exploring Similarity and Enlargement (25 min)
Discuss how similar shapes maintain proportionality and how enlargement affects dimensions.
- **Strategies:** Visual aids like grid overlays to show enlargement.
- **CFU:** “What stays the same and what changes during enlargement?”
- **The Trap:** Forgetting that angles remain constant during enlargement.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Congruent means similar | Both involve shapes | Clarify size vs. proportionality |
| Enlarged shapes have larger angles | Visual illusion | Use examples to show angle constancy |

## Engagement Hooks
- Use local architecture or art that features tessellations or transformations to spark interest.

## Assessment Guidance
Assess using practical tasks where students must identify or create congruent shapes, enlarged figures, or tessellations. Include both written and hands-on tasks.
