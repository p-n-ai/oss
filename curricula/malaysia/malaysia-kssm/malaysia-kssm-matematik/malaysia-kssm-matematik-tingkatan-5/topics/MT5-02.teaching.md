#### Teaching Sequence & Strategy

##### 1. Introduction and Order (2.1.1 - 2.1.2)
* **Strategies:** Start with a real table, such as school inventory, class sales, or transport schedules. Ask students to strip away the labels and keep only the numbers inside brackets. Then train them to say the order aloud: rows first, columns second.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If a matrix has 3 rows and 2 columns, what is its order? Is it $3 \times 2$ or $2 \times 3$?"
* **The Trap:** Students often swap row and column order. Use the mnemonic "Rows first, Columns second" every single time.

##### 2. Equality, Addition, Subtraction and Scalar Multiplication (2.1.3, 2.2.1, 2.2.2)
* **Strategies:** Treat matrix equality like checking twins: same order, same corresponding elements. Treat addition and subtraction like matching socks, where every element must have a partner in the same position. For scalar multiplication, explain that the outside number visits every element inside.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "Can a $2 \times 2$ matrix be added to a $2 \times 3$ matrix? Why not?"
* **The Trap:** Students add elements that are not in matching positions, or they forget to multiply every entry by the scalar.

##### 3. Matrix Multiplication - Boss Level (2.2.3)
* **Strategies:** Use the Visual 7 method. The first row of Matrix A dives into the first column of Matrix B. Make students say the inner dimensions before multiplying and predict the order of the answer first.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If I multiply a $(2 \times 3)$ matrix by a $(3 \times 1)$ matrix, is the operation possible, and what is the final order?"
* **The Trap:** Students multiply corresponding elements like addition. Stop them immediately and repeat: Row times Column, not element by element.

##### 4. Identity and Inverse Matrices (2.2.4 - 2.2.5)
* **Strategies:** Introduce the identity matrix as the number 1 of the matrix world because it leaves the matrix unchanged. For inverse matrices, teach the 2 x 2 switch-and-sign-flip pattern, then connect it to the determinant condition.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "If $ad - bc = 0$, what happens to the fraction $1/(ad-bc)$? Can the inverse exist?"
* **The Trap:** Students forget that the identity matrix has 1s on the main diagonal and 0s elsewhere, or they assume every square matrix has an inverse.

##### 5. Simultaneous Equations and Problem Solving (2.2.6 - 2.2.7)
* **Strategies:** Show the magic of $X = A^{-1}B$. Present it like a key unlocking unknown values. Use cost tables, sales data, or inventory records so students can see why matrices matter beyond mechanical calculation.
* **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "In $AX = B$, when we multiply by $A^{-1}$, does it go in front of $B$ or behind $B$?"
* **The Trap:** Students place the inverse on the wrong side or forget that matrix multiplication is not commutative, so $A^{-1}B$ is not the same as $BA^{-1}$.

#### High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | Localized Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Matrices of different orders can be added | Students think any two matrices can be combined. | Use the line: "Same size or no dice." Show the unmatched element that has no partner. |
| $AB = BA$ for all matrices | Students assume matrix multiplication behaves like ordinary numbers. | Compare a $2 \times 1$ and $1 \times 2$ example and show the results are completely different. |
| Identity matrix can have random diagonal values | Students remember only that the diagonal matters, but not the exact pattern. | Repeat the visual rule: main diagonal is all 1s, everything else is 0. |
| Every square matrix has an inverse | Students ignore the determinant condition. | Drill the checkpoint: if determinant = 0, the matrix is singular and has no inverse. |

#### Engagement Hooks
* **Cryptography Mode:** Show how secret messages can be encoded and decoded using a matrix key.
* **Big Data Power:** Discuss how recommendation systems and AI use giant matrix operations to process information fast.
* **Financial Context:** Use stock records, cost tables, or school canteen sales to connect matrices with budgeting and profit analysis.

#### Assessment Guidance & Projek Mini
* **Exam Focus:** Expect routine items on matrix operations, inverse matrices, and simultaneous equations using matrix methods.
* **Reasoning Focus:** Stronger students should explain why a multiplication is not possible or why an inverse does not exist.
* **Chatbot Projek Mini (TP6):** **"Room Inventory Hack."** Ask the student to list two categories of items in their room, such as books and stationery, then build an inventory matrix and a price matrix. They multiply the matrices and report the total value of their shelf or study corner.
