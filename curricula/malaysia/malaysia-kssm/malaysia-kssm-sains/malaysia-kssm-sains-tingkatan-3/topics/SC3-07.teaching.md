# Tenaga dan Kuasa — Teaching Notes

## Overview
The topic "Tenaga dan Kuasa" involves understanding the concepts of work, energy, and power, and how they relate to each other in various real-life contexts. Students will explore different types of energy such as gravitational potential energy, elastic potential energy, and kinetic energy, and learn how these energies transform within a closed system.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of force and motion, including concepts like speed and acceleration. Familiarity with basic mathematical operations such as multiplication and division is essential.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Work and Energy (15 min)
Introduce the concepts of work and energy with simple definitions and examples from daily life, such as lifting a book or using a toaster.
- **Strategies:** Use everyday objects to demonstrate work done, such as lifting a bag.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What happens when you push a door open? How does that relate to work?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing energy with force. Emphasize that energy is the ability to do work, not the force applied.

### 2. Gravitational and Elastic Potential Energy (20 min)
Discuss potential energy types and their real-world applications, such as a roller coaster's height and a stretched rubber band.
- **Strategies:** Use diagrams and animations to visually explain energy storage.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What happens to energy when you stretch a rubber band?"
- **The Trap:** Misunderstanding that potential energy is only present when objects move vertically.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Energy is only present when objects move | Students often equate energy with motion | Use examples of potential energy at rest, like a book on a shelf |
| Power and energy are the same | Both involve work, leading to confusion | Highlight that power is the rate at which work is done |

## Engagement Hooks
- Relate energy concepts to local scenarios, such as the energy used in traditional Malaysian games or appliances in a typical household.

## Assessment Guidance
Use a mix of qualitative questions and quantitative problems to assess understanding. Encourage students to explain energy transformations in their own words and solve basic energy calculations.
