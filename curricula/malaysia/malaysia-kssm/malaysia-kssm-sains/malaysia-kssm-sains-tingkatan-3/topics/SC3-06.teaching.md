# Keelektrikan dan Kemagnetan — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This unit explores the principles of electricity and magnetism, focusing on energy sources, electrical generation, and the role of transformers in electrical systems. Students will also learn about electrical safety in home wiring and energy efficiency.

## Curriculum Standards & Taxonomy
- **6.1.1 (Understand):** Describe renewable and non-renewable energy sources.
- **6.1.2 (Understand):** Explain energy generation processes with examples.
- **6.1.3 (Analyze):** Differentiate between direct current (DC) and alternating current (AC).
- **6.1.4 (Apply):** Solve problems related to electrical energy supply in daily life.
- **6.2.1 (Apply):** Conduct experiments to build step-up and step-down transformers.
- **6.2.2 (Understand):** Communicate the function of transformers in household electrical devices.
- **6.2.3 (Apply):** Solve transformer-related problems using formulas.
- **6.3.1 (Understand):** Explain components in electrical transmission and distribution systems visually.
- **6.3.2 (Understand):** Explain home electrical supply and wiring systems with examples.
- **6.3.3 (Analyze):** Differentiate safety components in home wiring systems.
- **6.3.4 (Understand):** Communicate about safety in electrical transmission, distribution, and appliance usage.
- **6.4.1 (Remember):** Define energy efficiency.
- **6.4.2 (Remember):** List examples of energy-efficient technologies.
- **6.4.3 (Apply):** Determine electrical energy usage in appliances.
- **6.4.4 (Apply):** Relate electrical energy usage, power, and time to calculate appliance usage costs.
- **6.4.5 (Apply):** Conduct a home electrical energy usage cost audit for energy-saving measures.
- **6.4.6 (Understand):** Communicate methods for saving electrical energy usage.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of energy types, simple electrical circuits, and safety precautions when handling electrical devices.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Energy Sources (20 min)
Introduce the concept of renewable and non-renewable energy sources with examples like solar, wind, fossil fuels, and nuclear power.
- **Strategies:** Use videos or infographics to demonstrate energy sources.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask, "Can you name one renewable and one non-renewable energy source?"
- **The Trap:** Students may confuse renewable with non-renewable resources due to similar examples like biofuels.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| All energy is renewable | Misunderstanding that all energy can be replenished | Clarify with examples of finite resources |
| Transformers generate energy | Confusion between transformation and generation | Explain transformers only change voltage levels |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss local energy projects or initiatives, such as renewable energy plants in Malaysia, to make concepts relatable.

## Assessment Guidance
Assess understanding through practical experiments, quizzes on energy types, and problem-solving exercises on transformer usage and energy calculations.
