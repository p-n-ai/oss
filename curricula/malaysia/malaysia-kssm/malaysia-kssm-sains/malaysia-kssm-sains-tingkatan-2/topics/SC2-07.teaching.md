# Keleektrikan dan Kemagnetan — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic explores the fundamental concepts of electricity and magnetism, focusing on how they are related and applied in everyday life. Students will gain insight into electrostatics, electric currents, circuits, and the properties of magnets and electromagnets.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have basic knowledge of atoms, the concept of force, and how energy is transferred and transformed. Familiarity with simple circuits and basic measurement units will be beneficial.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Energy and Electrostatics (15 min)
Introduce the concept of energy and its forms, focusing on electrical energy and electrostatics.
- **Strategies:** Use visual aids like diagrams showing static electricity; demonstrate with a balloon and hair.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What happens to the balloon when rubbed on hair, and why?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing energy with force; clarify that energy is the ability to do work.

### 2. Understanding Electric Currents (20 min)
Explain how electric charges flow to create current, and introduce series and parallel circuits.
- **Strategies:** Use circuit diagrams and simulation software to visualize current flow.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "How does a switch control the flow of current in a circuit?"
- **The Trap:** Assuming current flows faster in parallel circuits; emphasize it's the same speed, but more pathways.

### 3. Exploring Magnetism and Electromagnets (25 min)
Discuss the properties of magnets and how electromagnets work.
- **Strategies:** Conduct hands-on experiments with magnets and coils.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What happens to a nail wrapped with a coil when current flows through it?"
- **The Trap:** Thinking that only metals can be magnets; clarify that not all metals are magnetic.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception                         | Why Students Think This                     | How to Fix                                     |
|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Static electricity is the same as current electricity | Both involve charges and seem similar     | Explain difference in movement of charges      |
| Magnets attract all metals            | Observations with common metals like iron   | Demonstrate with various metals and explain why some are non-magnetic |

## Engagement Hooks
- Connect to real-world applications like how refrigerators use magnets, or how static electricity affects daily life (e.g., clothes sticking together).

## Assessment Guidance
- Use quizzes focusing on definitions and applications of electricity and magnetism.
- Practical assessments through experiments like building simple circuits or creating an electromagnet.
