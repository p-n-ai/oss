# Nutrisi — Teaching Notes

## Overview
In this topic, students will explore the fascinating world of nutrition, understanding the different classes of food, and the importance of a balanced diet. They'll dive into food testing, caloric estimation, and the significance of healthy living. Additionally, the journey of food consumption will be examined, from digestion to absorption, and finally, excretion.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of human biology, particularly the major organ systems, and a rudimentary knowledge of food groups and their functions.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Kelas Makanan (30 min)
Discuss the six classes of food: carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, and water. 
- **Strategies:** Use food models, diagrams, and videos.
- **CFU:** "Can someone tell me why proteins are important for our body?"
- **The Trap:** Students might confuse carbohydrates with fats due to energy roles.

### 2. Ujian Kehadiran Nutrien (40 min)
Conduct experiments to test for starch, glucose, proteins, and fats.
- **Strategies:** Hands-on lab work with iodine, Benedict's solution, Biuret test, and ethanol.
- **CFU:** "What happens when iodine is added to a starch solution?"
- **The Trap:** Misinterpreting color changes in chemical tests.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| All fats are bad | Media often portrays fats negatively | Explain the difference between healthy and unhealthy fats |
| Carbs lead to weight gain | Diet trends misrepresent carbs | Discuss the role of carbs in energy |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss local dishes and their nutritional content, linking them to the food classes and balanced diet concepts.

## Assessment Guidance
Use practical lab assessments and quizzes to ensure students can identify food classes and perform nutrient tests. Include discussions on caloric needs based on different lifestyles.
