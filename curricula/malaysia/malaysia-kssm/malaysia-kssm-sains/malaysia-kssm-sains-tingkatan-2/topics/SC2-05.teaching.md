# Air dan Larutan — Teaching Notes

## Overview
In this unit, students will explore the properties and significance of water and solutions in daily life. They'll learn about the concepts of solubility, colloids, and the importance of water as a universal solvent. Students will also engage in practical experiments to understand evaporation, solubility, and methods to ensure clean water supply.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of matter states (solid, liquid, gas) and simple mixtures. Basic lab safety protocols and prior experience with simple experiments will be beneficial.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Water (15 min)
Introduce water's unique properties, including its high heat capacity and surface tension.
- **Strategies:** Use a video of water's surface tension, demonstrate with small floating objects.
- **CFU:** "Why do you think water can hold up small objects without them sinking?"
- **The Trap:** Students might think all liquids behave like water; emphasize unique properties.

### 2. Evaporation Experiment (20 min)
Conduct a simple experiment to observe water evaporation.
- **Strategies:** Set up a small-scale evaporation experiment using shallow dishes of water.
- **CFU:** "What do you observe happening to the water level over time?"
- **The Trap:** Misunderstanding that evaporation only occurs at boiling; clarify through discussion.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Water boils at the same temperature everywhere | Lack of understanding of altitude effects | Explain boiling point changes with altitude |
| Solubility means a substance disappears | Visual misinterpretation | Demonstrate undissolved residue in saturated solutions |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss how local industries use water as a solvent (e.g., rubber processing, palm oil extraction).
- Connect evaporation to daily life examples like drying clothes or puddles disappearing after rain.

## Assessment Guidance
Use practical reports and observations to assess understanding of experiments. Quizzes can test theoretical knowledge, while group discussions can reveal misconceptions.
