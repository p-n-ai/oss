# Haba — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic introduces students to the concept of heat, its flow, and its impact on materials. Students will explore the differences between heat and temperature and understand how heat transfer occurs in nature and everyday life. The curriculum further delves into conduction and insulation, emphasizing practical applications and the behavior of materials when subjected to heat.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of states of matter (solid, liquid, gas) and the concept of temperature before starting this topic.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Understanding Heat and Temperature (20 min)
Introduce the concepts of heat and temperature. Use a simple analogy like comparing heat to the amount of water and temperature to the pressure of water in a hose.
- **Strategies:** Use thermometers and heat-sensitive materials for demonstrations.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask students to differentiate between heat and temperature in their own words.
- **The Trap:** Students often think heat and temperature are the same. Clarify by emphasizing that temperature measures the intensity of heat, not the quantity.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Heat and temperature are the same | Both involve thermal energy and are often used interchangeably in casual language | Use analogies and practical experiments to show differences |

## Engagement Hooks
- Relate heat transfer to cooking methods familiar to students, like grilling satay or boiling water for noodles, to make the concept relatable.

## Assessment Guidance
Assess understanding through practical experiments where students measure the temperature change in different materials and environments, ensuring they can explain the observed changes.
