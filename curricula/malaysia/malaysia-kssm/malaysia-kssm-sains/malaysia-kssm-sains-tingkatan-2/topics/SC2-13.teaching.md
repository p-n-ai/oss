# Meteoroid, Asteroid, Komet — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic introduces students to the fascinating celestial objects within our solar system: meteoroids, asteroids, and comets. Students will explore their characteristics, movements, and the potential impact they have on Earth. They'll also brainstorm ways to mitigate the risks associated with these objects.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a solid understanding of the solar system, including the sun, planets, and other celestial bodies, as covered in SC2-12: Sistem Suria.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Celestial Objects (20 min)
Start by defining and differentiating meteoroids, asteroids, and comets. Use visuals like diagrams or models to illustrate their differences.
- **Strategies:** Use a Venn diagram to compare and contrast.
- **CFU:** "Can anyone explain what makes a comet different from an asteroid?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing meteoroids with meteorites.

### 2. Movements and Impacts (25 min)
Discuss the orbits and movements of these celestial objects and their potential impacts on Earth. Highlight historical events.
- **Strategies:** Show videos of meteor showers and simulations of asteroid impacts.
- **CFU:** "Why do you think some asteroids pose a greater threat to Earth than others?"
- **The Trap:** Believing all asteroids are on a collision course with Earth.

### 3. Mitigation Strategies (30 min)
Encourage creative thinking on how to prevent or minimize asteroid collisions with Earth. Discuss current technologies and future possibilities.
- **Strategies:** Group brainstorming sessions.
- **CFU:** "What are some innovative ways we could prevent an asteroid impact?"
- **The Trap:** Assuming that we have no control over these celestial threats.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Meteoroid vs Meteorite | They sound similar | Clarify definitions with examples |
| All comets have tails | Seen in images | Explain tail formation depends on proximity to the sun |
| Asteroids are all large rocks | Media portrayal | Discuss varying sizes and compositions |

## Engagement Hooks
- Relate to the recent meteor shower events or news about asteroid flybys visible from Malaysia. Discuss the cultural significance of comets in local folklore.

## Assessment Guidance
Assess understanding through creative projects, such as designing a comic strip showing the journey of a comet, or a presentation on new asteroid deflection technologies.
