# Nutrisi dan Teknologi Makanan — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This unit dives into the essential understanding of balanced nutrition and the role of both macro and micronutrients in plants and humans. Students will explore the nutritional value of foods, how calories are measured, and the consequences of dietary imbalances. We'll also discuss food technology, including processing techniques and the impact of health supplements and Halal status on food choices.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have basic knowledge of human and plant biology, particularly concerning dietary needs and plant growth. Familiarity with basic experimental procedures and data interpretation is also necessary.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Balanced Nutrition (45 min)
Introduce the concept of a balanced diet and its importance for health.
- **Strategies:** Use food pyramid visuals and dietary guidelines.
- **CFU:** "What makes a meal balanced?"
- **The Trap:** Students may confuse calorie count with nutritional value.

### 2. Caloric Measurements (50 min)
Conduct experiments to estimate calorie content in food samples.
- **Strategies:** Demonstrations using calorimeters, group lab activities.
- **CFU:** "How do we calculate the energy in food?"
- **The Trap:** Misinterpretation of experimental results due to procedural errors.

### 3. Nutrient Functions (40 min)
Discuss macro and micronutrients, emphasizing their roles in plants.
- **Strategies:** Diagrams of nutrient cycles, interactive discussions.
- **CFU:** "Why are certain nutrients vital for plant health?"
- **The Trap:** Overlooking the difference between macro and micronutrients.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| All fats are bad | Media often portrays fats negatively | Explain different types of fats and their roles |
| Plants only need water and sunlight | Simplified primary education concepts | Highlight nutrient uptake and soil health |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss local dietary habits and relate them to balanced nutrition.
- Use case studies from Malaysian agriculture to illustrate nutrient importance.

## Assessment Guidance
Assess students through practical experiments, short quizzes on nutrient functions, and group presentations on food technology topics. Encourage peer review to foster collaborative learning.
