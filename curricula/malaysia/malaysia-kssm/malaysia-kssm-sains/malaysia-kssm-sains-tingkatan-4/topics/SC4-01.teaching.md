# Langkah Keselamatan di dalam Makmal — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic focuses on lab safety measures that are crucial for all students working in a science lab. Emphasis is placed on understanding personal protective equipment, waste management, accident response, and fire safety. The knowledge gained here is foundational for ensuring a safe learning environment in practical science sessions.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have basic knowledge of the lab environment and a general understanding of potential hazards in a laboratory setting. Familiarity with common laboratory equipment and their uses is also beneficial.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) (15 min)
Introduce PPE, such as goggles, gloves, and lab coats, and explain their importance in protecting against injuries.
- **Strategies:** Use real examples of PPE for demonstration. Show short videos of PPE usage.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask students why each piece of equipment is important.
- **The Trap:** Students might think PPE is only for chemical spills. Emphasize that it's also for biological and physical hazards.

### 2. Waste Management (20 min)
Discuss materials safe for sink disposal and those that aren't, and how to handle biological waste.
- **Strategies:** Use a sorting activity with various materials to classify them.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask students to list items they think can be disposed of in the sink.
- **The Trap:** Confusing biodegradable waste with sink-safe waste. Clarify the difference.

### 3. Accident Management (15 min)
Outline steps to manage accidents, including reporting and initial response actions.
- **Strategies:** Role-play scenarios to practice responses.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask what should be done first when an accident occurs.
- **The Trap:** Students may panic or forget steps. Teach calmness and order.

### 4. Fire Safety (25 min)
Explain fire extinguisher types, usage, and conduct a school audit.
- **Strategies:** Demonstrate the PASS method (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep) with a mock extinguisher.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Have students explain the PASS method in their own words.
- **The Trap:** Not recognizing different fire types. Reinforce matching extinguisher types to fires.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| PPE is optional for minor tasks | Lack of immediate consequences | Stress PPE's role in preventing long-term issues |
| All liquids can go down the sink | Sink is seen as a catch-all | Emphasize environmental impact and safety regulations |

## Engagement Hooks
- Use a local news story about a lab accident to highlight the importance of safety measures.

## Assessment Guidance
- Observe practical demonstrations of PPE use, waste sorting, and fire extinguisher application.
- Use quizzes with scenarios to test students' understanding of accident management.
