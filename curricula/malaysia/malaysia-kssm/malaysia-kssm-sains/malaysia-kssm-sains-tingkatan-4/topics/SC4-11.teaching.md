# Daya dan Gerakan — Teaching Notes

## Overview
In this topic, students will explore the fundamental concepts of force and motion. They will learn the differences between distance and displacement, understand speed, average speed, velocity, and acceleration, and apply these concepts to solve real-world problems. Additionally, they will conduct experiments to understand gravitational acceleration and explore the concepts of mass and inertia.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding of measurement units, be familiar with graphs, and have some prior knowledge of basic physics concepts such as gravity and motion.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Distance and Displacement (15 min)
- **Teaching Instructions:** Start by explaining distance as the total path traveled and displacement as the shortest path between two points.
- **Strategies:** Use a simple map or diagram to illustrate the path versus the shortest route.
- **CFU:** "Can you describe the difference between walking around the block and cutting through a park?"
- **The Trap:** Students often confuse distance with displacement, thinking they are the same.

### 2. Understanding Speed and Velocity (20 min)
- **Teaching Instructions:** Clarify that speed is scalar (no direction) while velocity is vector (includes direction).
- **Strategies:** Use a toy car to demonstrate speed and direction changes.
- **CFU:** "What happens to velocity if the car changes direction but maintains speed?"
- **The Trap:** Misunderstanding that speed and velocity are interchangeable.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Distance and displacement are the same | They both involve movement | Use diagrams to show different paths |
| Speed and velocity are identical | Both involve how fast something is moving | Emphasize directional aspect of velocity |

## Engagement Hooks
- Use examples like the speed of local transport or athletes to illustrate real-world applications of speed and velocity.

## Assessment Guidance
- Use practical problems that require calculations of speed, velocity, and acceleration.
- Graph interpretation exercises are crucial for assessing understanding of motion.
