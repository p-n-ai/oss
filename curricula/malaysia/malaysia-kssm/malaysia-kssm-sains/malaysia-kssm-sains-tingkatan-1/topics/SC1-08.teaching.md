# Cahaya dan Optik — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic explores the fascinating world of light and optics, focusing on how light behaves when it encounters different materials. Students will learn about concepts like reflection, refraction, and the formation of images through various types of mirrors. The practical applications of these concepts in everyday life will be highlighted to enhance understanding and appreciation.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a basic understanding that light travels in straight lines and the concept of reflection. Familiarity with basic geometry concepts like angles will be beneficial for understanding how light reflects and refracts.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Introduction to Light and Optics (15 min)
Introduce the fundamental nature of light, its speed, and how it travels in straight lines. Discuss the basic concepts of reflection and refraction.
- **Strategies:** Use a flashlight and mirror to demonstrate light reflection; show refraction with a glass of water and a pencil.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "What happens when light hits a mirror? Why does the pencil look bent in water?"
- **The Trap:** Students may think light only travels in straight lines without bending or reflecting.

### 2. Reflection of Light (30 min)
Explore how light reflects off surfaces, focusing on plane, concave, and convex mirrors. Discuss image formation in each type.
- **Strategies:** Use ray diagrams to illustrate image formation; hands-on activity with mirrors.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** "How does the image in a concave mirror differ from that in a convex mirror?"
- **The Trap:** Confusing the image properties between different mirror types.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Light travels in a straight line without bending | Observations in open spaces | Demonstrate refraction with water and glass |
| All mirrors create the same type of image | Lack of distinction between mirror types | Use ray diagrams and real-life examples |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss local uses of mirrors, such as concave mirrors in headlights and convex mirrors in parking structures, to highlight practical applications.

## Assessment Guidance
Encourage students to draw ray diagrams for various scenarios. Use quizzes and practical demonstrations to assess understanding of reflection and refraction concepts.
