# Bumi (SC1-09) — Teaching Notes

## Overview
This topic explores the intricate systems of Earth, its layers, types of rocks, geological processes, and geohazards. Students will delve into the geological time scale, understand fossils, and examine water systems and minerals, appreciating the unique conditions on Earth that foster life.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have a foundational understanding of Earth's structure, natural resources, and basic environmental issues. Familiarity with natural disasters will enhance comprehension of geohazards.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Earth's Layers and Systems (20 min)
Introduce Earth's layers and systems through diagrams and models.
- **Strategies:** Use cross-section models, videos of Earth's interior.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Ask students to label Earth's layers on a diagram.
- **The Trap:** Students may confuse the crust and mantle. Reinforce with cross-section visuals.

### 2. Rock Types and Formation (25 min)
Discuss types of rocks and their formation processes.
- **Strategies:** Bring rock samples for tactile learning, flowcharts for processes.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Have students categorize rock samples.
- **The Trap:** Students might mix up igneous and metamorphic rocks. Illustrate with clear examples.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception            | Why Students Think This       | How to Fix                                   |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| All rocks are the same   | Surface similarity            | Hands-on examination of samples              |
| Earth's core is solid    | Misunderstanding of pressure effects | Explain pressure and temperature roles  |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss local geological formations or recent natural events to connect the lesson with students' experiences.

## Assessment Guidance
Use project-based assessments where students model or simulate geological processes or present on local geohazards and their management.
