# Zaman Air Batu — Teaching Notes

## Overview
Zaman Air Batu, also known as the Ice Age, refers to periods in Earth's history when ice sheets covered significant portions of the planet. Understanding this era helps us recognize the dynamic nature of our planet and its impact on human civilization.

## Prerequisites Check
Students should have foundational knowledge of the concept of history and its relevance to understanding the present and future (SJ1-01: Mengenali Sejarah).

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Mengenali Dunia Kita (15 min)
Introduce the concept of Zaman Air Batu and its significance.
- **Strategies:** Use a world map to show ice coverage during Ice Ages, employ storytelling to depict life in these periods.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** What do you think life was like during the Ice Age?
- **The Trap:** Confusing Ice Age with the extinction of dinosaurs.

### 2. Menyenaraikan Zaman Air Batu (10 min)
Guide students in identifying different Ice Ages.
- **Strategies:** Use a timeline chart and historical images.
- **Check for Understanding (CFU):** Can you name one Ice Age period?
- **The Trap:** Believing Ice Ages occurred back-to-back without warm periods.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| Ice Age is one continuous period | Media often presents Ice Age as one era | Explain the cyclical nature of glacial and interglacial periods |
| Dinosaurs lived during the Ice Age | Movies and cartoons often depict this | Clarify timelines using a visual timeline |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss how climate change today is a modern "Ice Age" conversation starter, using local examples of weather changes.

## Assessment Guidance
- Encourage students to create a simple timeline and identify key characteristics of each Ice Age period.
- Use reflection questions to assess understanding of the impact on current climates and environments.
