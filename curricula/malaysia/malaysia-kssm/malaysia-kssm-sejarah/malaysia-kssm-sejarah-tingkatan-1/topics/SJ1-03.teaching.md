# Zaman Prasejarah — Teaching Notes

## Overview
Zaman Prasejarah merujuk kepada tempoh masa sebelum adanya rekod tulisan atau sejarah bertulis. Ia meliputi pelbagai era seperti Zaman Batu dan Zaman Logam, di mana manusia mula belajar membuat alat, bercucuk tanam, dan membina penempatan. Kajian terhadap zaman ini membantu kita memahami asas kehidupan manusia dan evolusi budaya.

## Prerequisites Check
Before starting this topic, students should know:
- The concept of Zaman Air Batu and its significance in shaping early human history.

## Teaching Sequence & Strategy

### 1. Pengenalan kepada Zaman Prasejarah (15 min)
Introduce the concept of Zaman Prasejarah and its significance.
- **Strategies:** Use timelines and visual aids to show the progression from Zaman Air Batu to Zaman Prasejarah.
- **CFU:** "What does Zaman Prasejarah mean to you?"
- **The Trap:** Students may confuse "prehistory" with "ancient history."

### 2. Lokasi dan Ciri Kehidupan (20 min)
Discuss the geographical spread and lifestyle during the Zaman Prasejarah.
- **Strategies:** Use maps to pinpoint locations and role-play activities to illustrate daily life.
- **CFU:** "How did prehistoric humans adapt to their environment?"
- **The Trap:** Misunderstanding that all prehistoric people lived in caves.

### 3. Kesinambungan dan Sumbangan (15 min)
Explain the contributions of Zaman Prasejarah to modern life.
- **Strategies:** Use examples of tools and techniques that have evolved.
- **CFU:** "Can you name a tool from then that we still use today?"
- **The Trap:** Thinking prehistoric contributions are irrelevant today.

### 4. Bukti Tinggalan di Malaysia (30 min)
Explore archaeological findings in Malaysia.
- **Strategies:** Show pictures or replicas of artifacts found in Malaysia.
- **CFU:** "What do these artifacts tell us about our ancestors?"
- **The Trap:** Assuming all artifacts are from the same period.

## High Alert Misconceptions
| Misconception | Why Students Think This | How to Fix |
|---------------|-------------------------|------------|
| All prehistoric people lived in caves | Media portrayal of cavemen | Showcase diverse living arrangements |
| Prehistory has no relevance today | Lack of visible connections | Highlight modern tools' evolution from prehistoric ones |

## Engagement Hooks
- Discuss the discovery of a local prehistoric site or artifact to create a personal connection.
- Invite a local archaeologist for a talk or virtual tour of a museum's prehistoric section.

## Assessment Guidance
- Use both formative and summative assessments, such as quizzes and project-based assessments, to gauge understanding.
- Encourage group discussions and presentations to assess analytical and creative skills.
